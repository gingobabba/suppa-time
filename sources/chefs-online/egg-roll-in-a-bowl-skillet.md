### Ingredients
- 1 cup jasmine rice, rinsed
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 pound ground pork
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 inch ginger root, peeled and grated (or 1 teaspoon ground ginger)
- 1/4 cup coconut aminos (or reduced-sodium tamari or reduced-sodium soy sauce)
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon sriracha
- 9 ounces bag coleslaw mix (or ~1/2 head of cabbage, thinly sliced and 3/4 cup shredded carrots)
- 6 green onions, cut into 1-inch pieces
- roughly chopped cilantro, to garnish
- 1 lime, quartered, for serving

### Method
1. Cook the rice: Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan. Mix in the rice, reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until the water is absorbed and rice is fluffy, about 15 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, heat the olive oil over medium heat in a large, deep skillet. Once the oil is shimmering, add the ground pork and cook, stirring occasionally and breaking into small pieces until no pink remains, 6 to 8 minutes. Stir in the garlic and ginger and cook, stirring often, until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
3. Add the coconut aminos, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and sriracha. Mix to combine and bring the liquid to a simmer. Add the coleslaw mix and green onions and mix again. Cook, stirring occasionally until the cabbage and carrots are tender and the sauce has thickened slightly, about 5 minutes. Remove from the heat.
4. Evenly divide the cooked rice among four plates and top with even servings of the pork mixture. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with a lime wedge.
