### Ingredients
- canned chickpeas: 1 (15 oz) can
- large head of cauliflower, cut into florets: 1
- Dijon mustard: 1 tablespoon
- garlic powder: ½ teaspoon
- lemon, preferably organic: 1 large
- fresh dill: ½ cup packed
- cooked beets (store-bought ready-to-eat): 2 (8 oz) packages, quartered
- balsamic vinegar: 1 tablespoon
- goat cheese, crumbled: 2–4 oz
- toasted pistachios: ¼ cup
- chives, minced: 3 tablespoons
- sweet potatoes, scrubbed and halved: 2 medium (about 8 oz each)
- whole milk yogurt: 1 cup
- butter: 1 tablespoon
- pecans, chopped: 1 cup
- ground cumin: ⅛ teaspoon
- cayenne: tiny pinch
- honey: 2 teaspoons

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large baking sheet with parchment.
2. Drain and pat chickpeas dry. Add to baking sheet with cauliflower florets, ¼ cup olive oil, Dijon, 1½ tsp salt, several grinds of pepper, garlic powder, lemon zest, and juice of ½ lemon. Toss well. Roast 30 minutes, stir, then roast another 15–25 minutes until deeply golden and tender. Top with dill and remaining lemon juice before serving.
3. Meanwhile, prep the sweet potatoes: coat halves with olive oil and salt, place cut-side down on a small baking sheet. Roast alongside the cauliflower for 45 minutes until very soft.
4. Make chive yogurt: blend yogurt, chives, ¼ tsp garlic powder, a pinch of salt, and juice of ½ lemon until smooth. Adjust seasoning to taste.
5. Make brown butter crunch: melt butter in a skillet over medium-high. Add pecans and stir until toasty, 3–5 minutes. Add ¼ tsp garlic powder, cumin, cayenne, a pinch of salt, and 2 tsp honey. Cook 10 seconds. Remove from heat.
6. Toss beets with balsamic, 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper. Top with goat cheese, pistachios, and chives.
7. Serve sweet potatoes topped with chive yogurt and brown butter crunch.

### Notes
Three dishes in one: roasted cauliflower & chickpeas, beet salad, and sweet potatoes. All roast at 425°F simultaneously.
