### Ingredients
- 14 ounce block firm tofu
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch, arrowroot starch, or tapioca flour
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 pound carrots, sliced into 1/2-inch pieces (about 3-4 large carrots)
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/3 cup coconut aminos or reduced sodium soy sauce
- 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
- 1 cup dry wild rice blend
- 3 green onions, sliced
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (optional)

### Method
1. Prepare the wild rice according to package instructions, be sure to rinse the rice well prior to cooking.
2. Meanwhile, press the tofu: If you do not have a tofu press, wrap the block in a clean towel or paper towels and place a heavy bottomed pan or baking dish on top for 20-30 minutes to remove the moisture. Once pressed, slice into 1/2-inch cubes.
3. Preheat the oven to 350F and line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
4. Prepare the tofu: Mix together the cubed tofu, cornstarch, salt, garlic powder, and ground black pepper in a large bowl until evenly coated. Set aside.
5. Prepare the carrots: In a separate bowl, add the carrots, olive oil, salt, garlic powder, and ground black pepper. Mix until evenly coated.
6. Transfer the coated tofu and carrots to the prepared sheet pan. Ensure the components are spread in one even layer. Bake until the tofu is beginning to brown and the carrots are fork tender, 20 to 25 minutes.
7. Meanwhile, prepare the sauce: whisk together all the sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
8. After 20-25 minutes, remove the sheet pan from the oven and pour the sauce evenly over the tofu and carrots. With a spatula, toss gently to coat in sauce. Bake until the tofu and carrots are crisp and caramelized, 10 to 20 minutes.
9. To serve, divide the wild rice, tofu and carrots evenly among 4 plates. Top with thinly sliced green onion and sesame seeds.
