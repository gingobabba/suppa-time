### Ingredients
- pasta of choice (e.g. cavatappi): 1 pound
- broccoli florets (or one 10 oz steam-in-bag bag): 2 cups
- boneless skinless chicken thighs: 1 pound
- extra-virgin olive oil: as needed
- lemons, ideally organic: 2
- salt and pepper: to taste
- full-fat cottage cheese: 1 cup
- grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, plus more for serving: ½ cup
- garlic cloves, peeled: 2
- packed basil leaves, plus more for serving: 1 cup
- packed spinach leaves: 1 cup
- milk of choice: ½ cup

### Method
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta about 1 minute less than package instructions. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining. Drain and set aside.
2. Steam broccoli florets until bright green and crisp-tender, about 5 minutes (steamer basket over pasta water, boiled in the same pot after the pasta, or microwaved in a steam-in-bag).
3. Pat chicken thighs dry, drizzle with EVOO, season both sides generously with salt and lots of pepper. Zest 1 lemon evenly over the chicken.
4. Heat a large cast-iron or non-stick skillet over medium-high heat (no oil needed). Add chicken thighs and cook 4 minutes. Flip when golden brown. Cook another 4–5 minutes until cooked through (165°F). Squeeze the zested lemon over the cooked chicken and let rest.
5. Blend steamed broccoli, cottage cheese, Parm, garlic, basil, spinach, milk, a large pinch of salt, and the zest and juice of the second lemon until smooth. Add more milk if too thick.
6. Return pasta pot to medium heat. Add broccoli pesto and ¼ cup pasta water. Toss to coat until warm and glossy, about 2 minutes. Add more pasta water to loosen if needed. Season to taste.
7. Serve pasta topped with sliced chicken, freshly grated Parm, basil leaves, a drizzle of EVOO, a squeeze of lemon, and flaky salt.

### Notes

Broccoli pesto sauce doubles as a great sauce for other dishes. Serves 4–6.
