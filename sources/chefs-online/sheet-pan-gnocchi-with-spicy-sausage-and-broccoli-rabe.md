### Ingredients
- 12 ounces frozen gnocchi (I use frozen gluten-free cauliflower gnocchi)
- 8 ounces broccolini, chopped into 1 1/2-inch pieces
- 2 packed cups chopped kale
- 1 shallot, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
- 1 pound ground spicy Italian pork or chicken sausage
- juice of 1 lemon
- 4 ounces crumbled goat cheese, for topping (optional)

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 400F. Prepare a large sheet pan (option to line with parchment paper).
2. Add the gnocchi, broccolini, kale, shallot, olive oil, garlic powder, salt, ground black pepper, and red pepper flakes to the sheet pan. Toss to coat the gnocchi and vegetables evenly with the oil and spices, taking care to massage the oil into the kale so it doesn’t burn, and spread everything out in one even layer.
3. With lightly oiled hands, shape the ground sausage into 16 to 18 evenly sized meatballs, and place them on the sheet pan, clearing small openings between the gnocchi and vegetables.
4. Bake until the meatballs reach an internal temperature of 165F and the vegetables and gnocchi are crisp, 17 to 22 minutes.
5. Remove the sheet pan from the oven and squeeze the lemon juice and sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese over top. Divide evenly among 4 plates to serve.
