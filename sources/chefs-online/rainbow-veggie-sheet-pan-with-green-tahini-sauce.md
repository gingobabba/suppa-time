### Ingredients
- 1 cup diced sweet potato (about 1 large or 2 medium sweet potatoes), 1/2-inch dice
- 1 cup sliced red bell pepper, 1/4-inch thick
- 1/2 large head of broccoli, chopped into small florets (about 3 cups)
- 1/3 purple cabbage head, thinly sliced (about 3 cups)
- 2 cans chickpeas, drained, rinsed, u0026 dried with a paper towel
- 2-3 tbsp olive or avocado oil
- 1 tsp kosher salt + additional to taste
- 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
- 2 tsp garlic powder
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 2/3 cup fresh chopped cilantro, packed (stems removed)
- 2/3 cup fresh chopped parsley, packed (stems removed)
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 1/3 cup creamy tahini
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt + more to taste
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 2 tbsp pure maple syrup

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 400F and line a large sheet pan with parchment paper. You may need two separate sheet pans for this recipe if a large one isn't accessible.
2. Combine the salt, ground black pepper, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and oregano in a small bowl and set aside.
3. Place the chopped sweet potato, bell pepper, broccoli, cabbage, and drained chickpeas to the sheet pan in separate rows. Evenly drizzle the olive oil and spice mix over the veggies and chickpeas.
4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until the sweet potatoes are just tender to a fork. Then toss to mix and place bake for another 15-25 minutes, until the veggies are crisp and browned.
5. While the veggies bake, make the tahini sauce by processing the cilantro, parsley, and olive oil in a food processor until the herbs are chopped into small pieces. Add the tahini, 1/2 cup of water, the salt, garlic powder, and maple syrup and process again until smooth, 30 to 60 seconds. Add up to 1 cup of additional water, in 1/4 cup increments, as needed based on your desired sauce consistency.
6. Evenly divide the roasted veggie mix among 4 bowls and drizzle with green tahini sauce. For additional protein, serve on top of a serving of cooked quinoa.
