### Ingredients
- 3/4 cup raw cashews
- 1 1/2 cups boiling water
- 1/3 broccoli head, cut into small florets (about 2 cups)
- 2 ounces baby spinach
- 16 ounces gluten-free spaghetti
- 1/2 cup low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, additional to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 3 tablespoons nutritional yeast
- 1/4 cup raw cashews
- 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes

### Method
1. Place the cashews in a glass bowl or jar and cover completely with the hot water. Set aside for 20-30 minutes to soak the cashews.
2. Meanwhile, boil a pot of salted water. Once at a roaring boil, add the broccoli and baby spinach. Cook for 5 minutes, then remove from the water and set aside.
3. Boil another pot of salted water and cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
4. Drain the soaked cashews, reserving all of the water. Add the broccoli, spinach, cashews, broth, lemon juice, and olive oil to a food processor or blender and blend until smooth. If needed, slowly pour in the leftover water from the cashews to thin out as needed. The sauce should be think enough to stick to the pasta, but smooth.
5. Add in the salt, black pepper, garlic powder, oregano, and nutritional yeast and blend again. Taste and salt accordingly. In the same pot as the pasta or a large bowl, add the sauce, starting with half of the total yield, and mix to combine. Add more sauce as desired.
6. To a clean blender or food processor, add the cashews, nutritional yeast, oregano, salt, and red pepper flakes and pulse until finely ground, similar to the texture of parmesan cheese.
7. Evenly divide the pasta among 4 bowls and top with the cashew “parm.”
