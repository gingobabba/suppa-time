### Ingredients
- 1 pound chicken breast
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 medium red onion, thinly sliced
- 3/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 2 tablespoons maple syrup
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
- 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/4 cup coconut aminos (or reduced-sodium tamari or reduced-sodium soy sauce)
- 1/4 cup tomato paste
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 3/4 cups 1:1 gluten-free baking flour or all-purpose flour, plus more for shaping
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 1/4 cups whole-milk or low-fat Greek yogurt
- 8 ounces shredded cheese (mozzarella or cheddar) (about 1 1/2 cups)

### Method
1. Preheat the oven to 400ºF. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Prepare the shredded chicken: Add the chicken, garlic powder, salt, black pepper, and onion powder to a large bowl. Mix to combine, then transfer to the prepared sheet pan. Bake until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F, about 25 minutes. Transfer the chicken to a large bowl and shred with two forks or with an electric mixer. See notes for slow cooker instructions.
3. Prepare the pickled red onions: Pack the onion slices into a 16-ounce heatproof glass jar or lidded container. In a small saucepan, combine 3/4 cup water, the apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, and salt and bring to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, remove from the heat. Carefully pour the hot vinegar mixture over the onions in the jar. Use a spoon to push down any uncovered onions. Let the onions sit uncovered until they reach room temperature, 25 to 30 minutes. Tightly seal then refrigerate.
4. Prepare the maple barbecue sauce: In a small bowl or jar, combine the chili powder, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper. Add the coconut aminos, tomato paste, vinegar, maple syrup, and oil and whisk rapidly until smooth, about 30 seconds. Set aside.
5. When the chicken is done, increase the oven temperature to 425ºF. Position an oven rack in the lower third of the oven. Set aside a large sheet pan and large piece of parchment paper.
6. Make the dough: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, garlic powder, and salt. Fold in the yogurt with a rubber spatula until just combined. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface with clean, lightly floured hands until the yogurt is fully incorporated and the dough is soft but not sticky, 2 to 3 minutes. If the dough feels too sticky to handle, add more flour by the tablespoon until you can form a ball.
7. To roll the dough, place the parchment paper on the counter and lightly sprinkle with flour. With a lightly floured rolling pin or floured hands, roll or press the dough on the parchment into a thin 13x10-inch rectangle, adding a bit more flour if the dough is still sticky. Transfer the parchment to the sheet pan.
8. Bake until the top is lightly browned, 15 to 17 minutes. Remove from the oven, but leave the oven on.
9. Top the pizza: Spread about 2/3 cup of the barbecue sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a 1/2- to 1-inch border on all sides. Top with the shredded chicken and cheese, evenly distributing throughout. Drizzle the remaining 1/3 cup of barbecue sauce on top.
10. Return to the oven and bake until the cheese has melted, about 10 minutes. Cool on the sheet pan for about 5 minutes, and top with pickled onions. Slice into four or eight even pieces to serve.
