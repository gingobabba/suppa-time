### Ingredients
- 15 ounces chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 10 ounces baby bella mushrooms, thinly sliced
- 1/2 jalapeno pepper, seeded and halved lengthwise
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 2 teaspoons chili powder
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- 12 six-inch corn tortillas
- roughly chopped cilantro, for topping
- 1/4 cup canned full-fat coconut milk
- 1/2 jalapeno pepper, seeded and chopped
- 1 garlic clove
- juice and zest of 1 lime
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon maple syrup
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, additional to taste
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 cups thinly sliced cabbage (or 9 ounces pre-shredded cabbage or coleslaw mix)

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 375F. Prepare a large sheet pan.
2. Roast the vegetables: Add the chickpeas and sliced mushrooms to a mixing bowl and toss with the olive oil, salt, chili powder, cumin, and garlic powder until fully coated. Transfer to the sheet pan. Add the jalapeno pepper half to the center of the pan face down. Bake until the chickpeas and vegetables are lightly browned and soft, 15 to 20 minutes.
3. Make the slaw sauce: Add the coconut milk, jalapeno pepper, garlic, lime zest, lime juice, rice vinegar, maple syrup, and salt. Blend on high speed until smooth. While continuing to blend, slowly stream in the olive oil. Taste and add additional salt if desired.
4. Mix the slaw: Add the cabbage to a mixing bowl and toss with about 2/3 of the sauce. Add additional sauce as desired, then reserve the rest for topping the tacos.
5. Warm the tortillas: wrap the stack of tortillas in a damp paper towel and microwave for about a minute to steam. Alternatively, warm on a skillet with a light coating of oil.
6. Assemble the tacos: divide the tortillas between four plates and evenly divide the chickpea mix and slaw between them. Top with chopped cilantro and additional sauce, if reserved.

### Notes
Chickpeas: These protein-packed legumes get crispy and golden when roasted, taking on the smoky spices while adding a satisfying bite. They’re budget-friendly, hearty, and make a perfect plant-based taco filling. Baby bella mushrooms: Earthy, savory, and meaty in texture, sliced baby bellas soak up all the seasoning and roast beautifully alongside the chickpeas.
