### Ingredients
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- 2 tablespoons olive oil, (divided)
- 1 pound carrots, peeled and cut into 1/4-inch slices
- 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast
- 1 lemon, halved
- 5 ounces plain Greek yogurt (sub for dairy-free as needed)
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped cilantro, additional for garnishing
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 cup jasmine rice, cooked for serving

### Method
1. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Prepare a large sheet pan.
2. Make the spice mix: combine the salt, black pepper, garlic powder, oregano, and smoked paprika in a bowl, then evenly separate into two small bowls.
3. In a large bowl, toss to coat the carrots in 1 tablespoon of the oil and one bowl (half) of the spice mix. Transfer to the sheet pan.
4. Add the chicken to the same bowl with the remaining tablespoon of olive oil and the remaining spice mix. Toss to coat. Transfer to the sheet pan, spreading out the chicken and carrots so nothing is overlapping. Add the lemon halves, face down, on each end of the sheet pan. Bake until the carrots are lightly crisp on the outside and fork tender, and the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F, 25 to 30 minutes.
5. While the chicken bakes, prepare the rice according to package instructions.
6. Prepare the yogurt sauce: add the yogurt, cilantro, olive oil, black pepper, garlic powder, and salt to a bowl. Squeeze the roasted lemon halves into the bowl, then mix until evenly combined.
7. To serve, plate even servings of the cooked rice and top with carrots and chicken. Add a dollop of the yogurt sauce on top and garnish with chopped cilantro.

### Notes
: Lemon: A fresh lemon brings out the bold Mediterranean flavors of this dish. The leftover lemon half can be used to garnish when serving or juiced to help thin out the yogurt sauce. In a pinch? Opt for a lime or orange. Carrots: These carrots are packed with flavor and juices cooking alongside the chicken and evenly coated with the spice mix. If you&#8217;re low on time, you could also use a bag of baby carrots, or pre-sliced. Yogurt: Every good spiced sheet pan needs a cooling, creamy serving
