### Ingredients
- 4-5 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1/2” cubes
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp garlic powder
- 2 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp chili powder
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 15 oz can black beans, drained and rinsed
- 10-12 corn tortillas, plus olive oil and kosher salt to coat
- 1/2 cup fresh chopped cilantro (about 1/3 of a bunch)
- 3/4 cup full-fat coconut milk (half of a 13.5oz can)
- 4-6 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce (depending on preference)
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt, additional to taste
- juice of 1/2 lime

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 400F and line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Toss the cubed sweet potatoes in the olive oil, salt, garlic powder, cumin, chili powder, and oregano. Transfer to the sheet pan and bake for 40-50 minutes, tossing halfway through, until fork tender with crispy edges.
3. While the sweet potatoes cook, make the chipotle sauce by blending the coconut milk, chipotle peppers, salt, and lime juice in a blender or food processor on high speed until completely smooth. Set aside.
4. To prepare the tortillas, lightly drizzle each with olive oil, and use clean hands to cover the entire surface with oil. Stack 2-3 tortillas in a lightly damp paper towel and microwave for about 20 seconds, repeat the process for all 12 tortillas.
5. Transfer the softened tortillas to a separate, unlined, large sheet pan. Add about 1 tbsp of the chipotle sauce to the center of each tortilla. Place even servings of the cooked sweet potatoes and black beans on one half of the tortilla (don't over-stuff) then fold the tortilla closed. (If they're difficult to fold, they're likely over-stuffed.)
6. Reduce the oven to 375F and bake the tacos until crispy, 12-16 minutes. Remove from the oven and immediately sprinkle salt over the tacos. To serve, top each set of tacos with chopped cilantro and a side of chipotle sauce for dipping.
