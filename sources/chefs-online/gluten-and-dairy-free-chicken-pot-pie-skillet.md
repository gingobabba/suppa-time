### Ingredients
- 1 1/2 cups blanched almond flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
- 6 tablespoons unsalted vegan butter or unsalted butter, melted
- 3 large eggs
- flaky salt, for topping (optional)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 small yellow onion, small dice
- 3 large carrots, small dice
- 3 celery stalks, small dice
- 2 medium Yukon Gold potatoes, 1/4-inch dice (about 2 cups)
- 8 ounces white mushrooms, small dice
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/4 cup tapioca flour or arrowroot starch (can sub cornstarch)
- 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
- 1 teaspoon ground sage
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
- 3/4 cup canned full-fat coconut milk (about half of a well-shaken 13.5-ounce can)
- 3 cups shredded rotisserie chicken (or 1 pound boneless chicken breast, cooked and shredded)
- 1 cup fresh or frozen green peas

### Method
1. Make the biscuit dough: Add the almond flour, baking powder, thyme, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and rosemary to a large mixing bowl. Whisk to combine. Make a well in the center of the flour and crack in the eggs. Whisk until there are no streaks, then add the melted butter and stir to mix with the flour until just combined. Place the mixing bowl in the refrigerator to chill until Step 4.
2. Preheat the oven to 350ºF. Heat the olive oil in a large, oven-proof skillet (11 or 12-inch deep cast iron skillet works best) over medium heat.
3. Make the pot pie filling: Add the onion, carrots, and celery, and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, 7 to 9 minutes. Add the potatoes, mushrooms, and garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are just about fork tender, about 10 minutes but exact timing varies based on the size of the potatoes. Stir in the tapioca flour, salt, rosemary, sage, thyme, and black pepper and cook, stirring constantly, until fragrant and no dry flour remains, about 2 minutes. Stir in the chicken broth and coconut milk and bring to a simmer, then mix in the shredded chicken and peas.
4. Turn off the heat and carefully spoon or scoop the biscuit dough on top of the skillet, working one scoop at a time and evenly distributing on top of the chicken and vegetables, about 8 scoops total. Press down on each scoop with a rubber spatula to flatten. Sprinkle the tops with flaky salt (if using).
5. Bake until the biscuit dough is lightly browned on top, the potatoes are fork tender, and the filling is bubbling, 20 to 25 minutes.
6. Serve immediately between six plates for best results.
