### Ingredients
- 14 ounce block firm tofu
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 2 tablespoons coconut aminos or reduced sodium soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
- 12 ounces broccoli florets (about ½ medium head of broccoli)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/3 cup creamy peanut butter
- 1/4 cup coconut aminos or reduced sodium soy sauce
- 1.4 cup low sodium chicken or vegetable broth
- 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
- 1 cup dry jasmine rice
- 1/3 fresh chopped cilantro,

### Method
1. For the prep
2. Press the tofu: If you do not have a tofu press, wrap the tofu block in paper towels or a clean kitchen towel and place a heavy object, like a cast iron pan or pot, on top for 20-30 minutes to release the moisture.
3. Prepare the rice according to package instructions.
4. For the bowls
5. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 400F and line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
6. Cut the tofu block into 1/2-inch cubes. Add to a large bowl and coat with the olive oil, coconut aminos, salt, garlic powder, and ground black pepper. Transfer to the sheet pan in one even layer.
7. To the same bowl, add the broccoli florets, olive oil, salt, and ground black pepper. Transfer to the sheet pan with the tofu, ensuring everything is evenly spaced and not overlapping. Bake for 20-30 minutes, until the tofu and broccoli are crisp on the outside.
8. Meanwhile, make the peanut sauce: whisk together all of the sauce ingredients in a small bowl until smooth.
9. To serve, evenly divide the rice, tofu, and broccoli among 4 plates or bowls. Drizzle with peanut sauce and top with chopped cilantro.
