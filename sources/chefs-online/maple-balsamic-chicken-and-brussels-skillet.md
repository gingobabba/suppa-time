### Ingredients
- 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
- 1/4 cup coconut aminos (or reduced-sodium tamari or reduced-sodium soy sauce)
- 3 tablespoons pure maple syrup
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
- 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 pound ground chicken
- 10 ounces shaved brussels sprouts
- 4 green onions, thinly sliced, plus additional to garnish if desired
- optional for serving: 1 cup wild rice or wild rice blend, cooked according to package instructions (or quinoa, jasmine rice, or brown rice)

### Method
1. Make the Maple Balsamic Sauce: whisk the balsamic vinegar, coconut aminos, and maple syrup in a small bowl or jar. Cover and set aside.
2. Combine the garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, chili powder, and salt in a small bowl and set aside.
3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat until shimmering. Add the ground chicken and cook until no pink remains, about 5 minutes (it’s okay if the chicken is not fully cooked at this stage, it will continue cooking).
4. Clear an opening in the center of the skillet and add the shaved brussels sprouts and green onion. Continue cooking, stirring occasionally, until the brussels sprouts are softened, 3 to 5 minutes. Add the spice mixture and stir continuously until fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes.
5. Pour in the Maple Balsamic Sauce and bring to a simmer. Once it’s simmering, continue to cook, stirring occasionally to ensure the chicken and brussels are fully coated, for 5 minutes.
6. To serve, divide evenly among four plates with a cooked grain of choice (if using), and top with green onion.
