### Ingredients
- 1 1/2 pounds salmon, cut into 1-inch cubes (skinless if desired)
- 1/3 cup coconut aminos (can substitute reduced-sodium tamari or reduced-sodium soy sauce)
- 3 tablespoons raw honey
- 2 tablespoons sriracha
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
- 3 garlic cloves, grated
- 10-12 ounces red radishes, very thinly sliced
- 1 English cucumber, very thinly sliced
- 6 thinly sliced green onions, plus more for topping
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 2 teaspoons raw honey
- 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
- kosher salt
- cup jasmine rice
- roughly chopped cilantro leaves
- sesame seeds
- 1 lime, quartered
- other optional serving components: shelled edamame, sliced avocado, seaweed sheets, furikake, kimchi

### Method
1. Preheat the oven to 400ºF. Line a 9x13 baking dish with parchment paper (if needed).
2. Add the salmon cubes to a large mixing bowl. Add the coconut aminos, honey, sriracha, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and garlic and mix to combine. Cover and refrigerate for 15 minutes to marinate.
3. Meanwhile, make the cucumber-radish salad: Add the radishes, cucumber, green onion, rice vinegar, honey, sesame oil, and a big pinch of salt to a medium mixing bowl and toss to combine. Chill in the refrigerator until step 5.
4. Prepare 1 cup of jasmine rice according to package instructions.
5. While the rice cooks, transfer the marinated salmon to the baking dish, including the excess liquid, without overcrowding. Bake until the salmon is light pink, 6 to 8 minutes, then turn the oven to high-broil and cook until the sauce is bubbling and the salmon are browned on top, another 3 to 4 minutes. Ensure the salmon reaches an internal temperature of 145ºF.
6. Assemble the bowls: Evenly divide the rice, edamame, cucumber-radish salad, and salmon bites between four bowls. Spoon liquid from the baking dish on top of the salmon. Top each bowl with roughly chopped cilantro, sesame seeds, additional green onion, and a squeeze of lime juice.

### Notes
: Salmon: I like to make this pescatarian-friendly bowl with Atlantic caught salmon because it’s meatier shape makes it ideal for cubing. You can make this meal with skin on salmon, or skinless depending on your preference, however be sure to not overcrowd the salmon on the sheet pan as it can lead to steaming the fish, leaving you without that crispy outer layer we are looking for.
