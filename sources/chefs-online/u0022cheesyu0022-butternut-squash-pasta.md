### Ingredients
- 3/4 cup raw cashews
- 12 oz gluten-free pasta of choice
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 yellow onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup vegetable broth
- 1 packed cup cooked butternut squash (see note for cooking instructions)
- 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
- 2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp ground sage
- Optional: crispy sage for topping (see note)

### Method
1. Add the cashews to a large glass jar or mixing bowl and cover with boiling water. Set aside to soak and soften for 30 minutes.
2. In the meantime, boil a pot of salted water and cook the pasta until al dente, reserving 1 cup of pasta water. Drain and rinse with cold water. Return to the pot, off of heat.
3. While the pasta cooks, heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and cook, stirring often, until translucent, 3 to 5 minutes. Stir in the garlic and cook for another minute, then remove from heat.
4. Add the soaked cashews to a blender or food processor along with the onion and garlic, broth, butternut squash, nutritional yeast, salt, pepper, and sage. Blend on high speed until completely smooth. If needed, add the reserved pasta water 2 tablespoons at time to thin.
5. Add the sauce to the pasta and mix to coat. Note: you may not want to use all of the sauce, depending on how sauced you prefer the pasta, so start with just over half and add from there.
6. Serve immediately for best results with crispy sage on top. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
