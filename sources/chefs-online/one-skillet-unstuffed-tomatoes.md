### Ingredients
- ground turkey (or any ground meat): 1 pound
- small yellow onion or large shallot, finely chopped: 1
- garlic cloves, minced: 3
- short-grain white rice (arborio, calrose, or sushi rice): 1 cup
- tomatoes, diced (or halved if cherry): 1½ pounds
- fresh basil or parsley, finely chopped: ¼ cup
- low-sodium chicken stock: 1½ cups
- panko breadcrumbs: 1 cup
- pesto: 2 tablespoons

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
2. Warm 2 tbsp olive oil in a 10-inch ovenproof skillet over medium heat. Cook onion and garlic 3–4 minutes until softened.
3. Add turkey, 1 tsp salt, and pepper. Increase heat to medium-high and cook, breaking meat into crumbles, until no longer pink.
4. Stir in rice, tomatoes, basil or parsley, and ¼ tsp salt. Stir in chicken stock and bring to a simmer over high heat. Turn off heat.
5. Cover the skillet (with a lid, baking sheet, or foil) and bake 30 minutes.
6. Meanwhile, stir together panko and pesto until crumbs are coated.
7. Check the rice — if still crunchy, cover and bake another 5–10 minutes.
8. Remove lid, spread pesto breadcrumbs over the top, and return to oven for 10 minutes until golden-brown.
9. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

### Notes
Any ovenproof skillet works — cast iron, nonstick, or stainless.
