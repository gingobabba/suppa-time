### Ingredients
- 4 medium sweet potatoes, halved lengthwise
- olive oil
- kosher salt
- olive oil
- 1 pound ground beef (85% lean recommended)
- 1 taco seasoning packet (3 1/2 to 4 tablespoons)
- salsa of choice
- 1 ripe avocado, pitted and mashed
- 1 lime, quartered
- roughly chopped cilantro leaves
- other optional toppings: shredded cheddar cheese, pickled jalapeños, pickled onions, hot sauce

### Method
1. Preheat the oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Place the halved sweet potatoes on the sheet pan and drizzle with olive oil and a few pinches of salt. Use clean hands to coat the sweet potatoes evenly with the oil and salt, then turn them cut side down on the pan, evenly spaced apart. Bake on the bottom rack of the oven until fork-tender and crisp, 28 to 30 minutes. Note: cook time can vary depending on size of potato.
3. Meanwhile, make the taco meat: heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat until shimmering. Add the ground beef and cook, stirring and breaking into small pieces until browned all over, 6 to 8 minutes.
4. Once the beef is browned and crumbled, mix in the taco seasoning, plus 1/3 cup of water. Bring to a simmer and stir until the seasoning is fully incorporated into the beef mixture. Remove from the heat.
5. Flip over the cooked sweet potatoes on the sheet pan and divide between four plates (2 halves per plate). Gently mash the sweet potato inside the skin and evenly divide the ground beef mixture on top. Top each half with salsa, mashed avocado, and any other toppings of choice. Squeeze the lime wedge over the top and sprinkle with chopped cilantro.
