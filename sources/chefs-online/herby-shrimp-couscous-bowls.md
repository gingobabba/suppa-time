### Ingredients
- shallot, thinly sliced: 1
- cumin: 1 teaspoon
- baby arugula or leafy herbs (cilantro and basil work well): 3 packed cups, divided
- lemon, preferably organic: 1
- extra-virgin olive oil: ¾ cup, divided
- large peeled and deveined shrimp: 1½ pounds
- mini seedless cucumbers (or 1 English cucumber): 2, cut into ½-inch pieces
- peaches, ripe but firm: 2, sliced ½-inch thick
- cherry tomatoes: ½ pint, halved
- feta cheese: 4 oz, sliced or crumbled
- couscous: 1½ cups

### Method
1. Make the herb sauce: add half the shallot, cumin, salt, pepper, and 2 cups of arugula/herbs to a blender. Add the juice of ½ lemon. Pulse until coarsely chopped. With blender on low, slowly pour in ½ cup olive oil. Taste and adjust seasoning.
2. Toss shrimp with ¼ cup herb sauce in a bowl. Set aside to marinate at room temp.
3. Make the salad: combine cucumber, peaches, tomatoes, and remaining shallot in a bowl. Add the juice of the other lemon half, feta, and 2 tbsp olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Bring 1½ cups water, 2 tbsp olive oil, and a pinch of salt to a boil. Remove from heat, stir in couscous, cover, and steam while you cook the shrimp.
5. Cook shrimp in a large skillet over medium-high heat, 2–3 minutes per side until pink, opaque, and curled into a U-shape. Season to taste.
6. Fluff couscous with a fork and toss with lemon zest and remaining 1 cup arugula/herbs. Season to taste.
7. Serve couscous topped with salad and shrimp. Drizzle with remaining herb sauce.
