### Ingredients
- 16 ouces cooked spaghetti, 2/3 cup pasta water reserved (I used Trader Joe's brown rice quinoa spaghetti)
- 2 cups fresh basil leaves, packed
- 3/4 cup garlic scapes, chopped into 1/2-inch pieces
- 1/2 cup pine nuts or walnuts
- 1/4 tablespoon hemp seeds
- 1 cup parmesan cheese (can sub for 1/3 cup nutritional yeast for dairy-free)
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt + additional to taste
- 1/2 cup olive oil (depending on thickness preference, you can do 1/2 cup to 1 cup)

### Method
1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions in a pot of salted water. Before draining, reserve 2/3 cup pasta water and set aside.
2. While the pasta cooks, make the pesto by processing the basil, garlic scapes, pine nuts, hemp seeds, parmesan, lemon juice, and salt in a food processor until broken into small pieces.
3. Scrape the sides of the food processor and process again to break down into as small of pieces as possible, then while continuing to run the processor slowly stream in the olive oil. Use between 1/2 cup and 1 cup of oil depending on your preference. For a thicker pesto, use closer to 1/2 cup. For a thinner pesto, use closer to 1 cup.
4. Add the pesto to the drained pasta in the same pot or a mixing bowl (start with half, then add more as you mix). Add the reserved pasta water as needed and mix well to coat all of the pasta in the pesto.
5. Once fully cooked, enjoy immediately or store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to four days.

### Notes
Garlic Scapes: Garlic scapes are the flowering stems of garlic plants, offering a milder, slightly sweet garlic flavor. They blend beautifully into pesto for a seasonal twist on the classic. Chopped into small pieces, they become tender and aromatic when processed. Basil Leaves: Fresh basil brings the signature herby brightness that makes pesto so vibrant and fragrant. Be sure to pack the leaves tightly when measuring to get the full flavor punch. Use sweet or Genovese basil for the best results
