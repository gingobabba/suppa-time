### Ingredients
- 3 ounces tomato paste
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 1/4 cup coconut aminos, soy sauce, or tamari
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
- pinch kosher salt and ground black pepper
- 4 salmon filets (about 1 pound total)
- 1/4 cup homemade barbecue sauce (recipe above)
- 12 ounces chopped or shredded kale
- olive oil, for drizzling
- pinch kosher salt
- 15 ounces sweet corn (or 2 to 3 fresh ears)
- 15 ounces black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 cup shredded carrots (about 3 large carrots)
- 1 avocado, pitted and cubed
- 1 cup chopped cilantro
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 3 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- Juice of 1 lime (about 3 tablespoons)
- homemade barbecue sauce, for drizzling (recipe above)

### Method
1. Preheat the oven to 400ºF. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Make the barbecue sauce: Whisk all of the sauce ingredients together in a medium bowl until smooth.
3. Transfer 1/4 cup of Barbecue sauce to a separate small bowl. Spread about 1 tablespoon of the transferred sauce on each salmon filet, covering the top. Transfer the filets to the prepared sheet pan and bake until an internal temperature of 145ºF, about 10 minutes.
4. Meanwhile, make the salad: Massage the kale with a drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of salt. Let it sit for 10 minutes to allow the kale to soften.
5. Add the corn, black beans, carrots, avocado, and cilantro, and toss to evenly combine. Add the olive oil, apple cider vinegar and lime juice and toss to combine.
6. To serve, evenly divide the salad among 4 plates or bowls. Top with a salmon filet on each. Drizzle with additional barbecue sauce, if desired.
