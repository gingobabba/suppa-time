### Ingredients
- 2 tbsp avocado or olive oil
- 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp chili powder
- 1 tsp oregano
- 1 shallot, finely chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 2/3 cup canned full-fat coconut milk
- juice of 1/2 lemon (~2 tbsp)
- 1 + 1/2 cups rice (I typically use RightRice for this recipe)
- 1 small jar sundried tomatoes in olive oil
- 2-3 cups baby spinach

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Turn a stovetop burner on and heat the oil over medium-high heat on a large skillet.
2. Pat the chicken breasts dry with a paper towel, then season generously with the salt, back pepper, paprika, chili powder and oregano.
3. Add the seasoned chicken to the skillet and cook for about five minutes on each side, until crisp and brown. Once the chicken is browned, remove from the skillet and set aside.
4. In the same skillet, add the chopped shallot and garlic and cook for a few minutes in the oil from the chicken, until browned and aromatic. For extra flavor, add in the olive oil from the sundried tomato jar.
5. Reduce heat to medium and pour in the broth, lemon juice and coconut milk. Mix to combine. Add in the rice, spinach and sundried tomatoes, and mix again to evenly distribute it.
6. Add the chicken back to the skillet on top of the rice mixture. Bake for 30-40 minutes, until the internal temperature of the chicken is 165 degrees.
7. For best results, serve immediately by scooping out the absorbed rice and placing the chicken on top. Top with chopped basil if you'd like. Store in the refrigerator in an airtight container for up to five days.
