### Ingredients
- zucchini, sliced into thin half circles: 2
- extra-virgin olive oil: as needed
- kosher salt: to taste
- chicken sausages (Italian, roasted garlic, or chicken & apple), sliced into ¼-inch rounds: 2
- cherry tomatoes, halved: 1 cup
- garlic cloves, minced: 2
- frozen or fresh white corn: 1½ cups
- black pepper: to taste
- balsamic vinegar: 1–2 tablespoons
- feta and fresh soft green herbs (optional, for topping): to taste

### Method
1. Cut zucchini in half vertically, then into thin half-circle slices.
2. Warm 1 tablespoon olive oil over medium-high heat in your largest skillet. Add zucchini in an even layer. Cook 4–5 minutes until golden-brown, stir, cook the other side another 3–4 minutes. Season with ½ teaspoon kosher salt.
3. While zucchini cooks, slice chicken sausages and halve cherry tomatoes.
4. Push zucchini to one side of the skillet off the flame. Add a drizzle of oil to the empty side, then add sausage and tomatoes. Brown all over, 4–5 minutes.
5. Mince garlic while sausage and tomatoes cook.
6. Add a little more oil if dry, then add garlic, corn, and several grinds of black pepper. Stir until corn is hot and excess moisture has evaporated, about 2 minutes.
7. Stir in 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar. Taste — add up to 1 more tablespoon for extra tanginess. Adjust salt as needed.
8. Divide between two plates or bowls. Garnish with feta and fresh herbs if desired.

### Notes

Serves 2 — doesn't feed a household of 3 without scaling. Flag when planning.
