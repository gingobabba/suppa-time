### Ingredients
- carrots, finely grated: 2
- large onion, finely chopped: 1
- celery stalk (including leaves), finely chopped: 1
- mild chilli pepper (or chilli flakes): 1
- white wine or Marsala wine: a splash
- oregano: 1 teaspoon
- concentrated tomato paste: 1 teaspoon
- cherry plum tomatoes (or tinned cherry plum tomatoes): 250g
- extra virgin olive oil: as needed
- salt and freshly ground pepper: to taste
- pasta of choice (e.g. trofie): to serve
- parmesan, for serving: generous amount

### Method
1. Finely grate the carrots, finely chop the onion, celery (including leaves), and chilli.
2. In a heavy-based pan, add plenty of extra virgin olive oil. On medium to low heat, sauté the onion and celery (careful not to fry or burn).
3. When the onion is translucent, add the carrots and grated garlic. Pour in a splash of white wine, let it evaporate, then add oregano, a pinch of salt, and a good grinding of black pepper.
4. When everything is soft, add the cherry plum tomatoes. If using tinned, add only the fruit for now.
5. Continue cooking until the sauce begins to caramelise but not burn. Add a couple of tablespoons of tinned tomato juice or water, and the concentrated tomato paste.
6. When the sauce is nearly ready, put pasta on to cook. Continue cooking the soffritto until vegetables have melted, adding pasta water or tomato juice as needed. The soffritto may take up to 40–45 minutes total.
7. Drain pasta and add to the soffritto sauce. Toss and serve with a generous amount of parmesan.
