### Ingredients
- 2 1/2 pounds boneless chicken thighs
- 1/3 cup coconut aminos, tamari, or reduced sodium soy sauce
- 1/4 cup sriracha, optional
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, additional to taste (reduce for tamari or soy sauce)
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- juice of 1 lime

### Method
1. Add the chicken thighs, coconut aminos, sriracha, honey, garlic, salt, paprika, chili powder, oregano, and onion powder to a 6-quart slow cooker. Use clean hands or tongs to mix until the chicken is coated in the spices and liquid.
2. Turn on the slow cooker, put the lid on, and set to low for 5 hours or high for 3 hours.
3. After the chicken is done cooking, use two forks to shred it up in the liquid. It should shred very easily at this point. Squeeze in the lime juice and salt to taste.
4. Enjoy immediately or store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days or freezer for a month.

### Notes
Chicken thighs: Juicy and flavorful, boneless chicken thighs are ideal for slow cooking because they stay tender and moist over long cook times. They absorb the bold flavors of the sauce while naturally adding richness to the dish. This cut is also more affordable than breasts, making it great for feeding a crowd.
