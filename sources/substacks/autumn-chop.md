### Ingredients

**Maple-balsamic dressing:**
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1/2 cup
- balsamic vinegar: 1/3 cup
- lemon juice: 2 tablespoons
- maple syrup: 1 tablespoon
- Dijon mustard (optional): 1 heaping teaspoon
- kosher salt: 3/4 teaspoon
- garlic powder: 1/4 teaspoon
- black pepper: to taste

**Salad:**
- red onion (large), sliced 1/4-inch thick: 1
- butternut squash, cubed into 1-inch pieces (fresh or frozen): 1 pound
- fresh thyme sprigs (or 3 rosemary sprigs): 6
- kosher salt and black pepper: to taste
- boneless, skinless chicken breasts: 2
- curly kale, de-ribbed and chopped (or 10–12 oz bag pre-chopped): 1 bunch
- Honeycrisp apple (large), thinly sliced: 1
- dates, chopped (or 1/4 cup dried cranberries or golden raisins): 5
- candied or roasted pecans or pistachios: 1/2 cup
- goat cheese or feta, crumbled: 8 ounces

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. Make the dressing: combine all dressing ingredients in a jar and shake until combined (or whisk in a bowl). Set aside.
3. Toss red onion, butternut squash, and herb sprigs with 2 tablespoons of the dressing and 1 teaspoon kosher salt on a rimmed sheet pan. Spread evenly and roast for 20 minutes.
4. Meanwhile, place chopped kale in a large serving bowl. Add 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt and massage with your hands 5–10 times until softened but not wet. Add the apple, dates, nuts, and cheese.
5. After 20 minutes, stir the vegetables. Add the chicken breasts to the pan, drizzle with a little dressing, and season generously with salt and pepper. Roast for 15–20 minutes until chicken reaches 160°F internally (it will carry over to 165°F as it rests) and squash is completely tender — you should be able to easily squish a piece with a fork. If the chicken is done before the squash, remove it and continue roasting the squash.
6. Let the chicken cool enough to handle, then chop into bite-sized pieces. Discard the herb sprigs. Add the chicken, squash, and onions to the salad bowl.
7. Add dressing a little at a time and toss to coat — you can always add more but can't take it off. Season to taste with salt and pepper and serve.

### Notes
Frozen butternut squash works — cook straight from frozen.
