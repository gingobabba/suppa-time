### Ingredients
- fennel bulb, stalks and root trimmed, thinly sliced: 1
- shallots, chopped: 2
- garlic cloves, smashed: 4
- cherry tomatoes: 2 pints (about 1½ pounds)
- feta block: 1 (8 oz)
- extra-virgin olive oil, divided: ¼ cup + 2 tablespoons
- kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper: to taste
- pork tenderloins (NOT pork loin): 2 (1 pound each)
- dried green herbs (oregano, dill, basil, parsley, herbs de Provence, etc.): 2 teaspoons
- Dijon mustard: 2 teaspoons
- basmati rice: 1½ cups
- boiling water: 2½ cups

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Add fennel, shallots, garlic, cherry tomatoes, feta block, ¼ cup olive oil, 1½ teaspoons salt, and several grinds of pepper to a 9x12-inch baking dish. Toss to coat.
3. Roast 45–55 minutes until tomatoes are collapsing and fennel and shallots are tender and beginning to brown.
4. Meanwhile, toss pork tenderloins with 2 tablespoons olive oil, dried herbs, Dijon, 2 teaspoons salt, and several grinds of pepper. Let marinate at room temperature.
5. Remove vegetables from oven and increase heat to 425°F. Bring 2½ cups water to a boil.
6. Sprinkle rice over the vegetables, then pour boiling water over top. Cover tightly with foil. Place pork tenderloins on top of the foil, about an inch apart. (Use a separate baking sheet if the foil is thin and might tear.)
7. Roast for 25 minutes total. Start checking the pork's internal temperature after 15 minutes — at 140°F, transfer to a cutting board and tent with foil to rest to 145°F.
8. After 25 minutes, remove the baking dish from the oven but leave the foil on for another 10 minutes to let the rice finish steaming.
9. Remove foil and stir the rice, breaking up tomatoes and feta. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and acidity (a splash of lemon juice or vinegar if the tomatoes were sweet).
10. Slice pork and serve over the rice. Garnish with fresh herbs or drizzle with pesto.

### Notes

90 min total, ~30 min active. Serves 4–6 — good weekend recipe.
