### Ingredients
- mild or spicy ground Italian sausage (links or bulk): 1 pound
- celery stalks, finely chopped: 3
- carrots, finely chopped: 3
- large yellow onion, finely chopped: 1
- unsalted butter: 4 tablespoons
- sliced mushrooms: 8 oz
- all-purpose flour: 2 tablespoons
- kosher salt: 1½ teaspoons
- black pepper: several grinds
- dried rosemary or Italian seasoning: ½ teaspoon
- smoked paprika: ½ teaspoon
- Cajun seasoning: ½ teaspoon
- low-sodium chicken stock (or water + Better Than Bouillon): 6 cups, plus up to 2 more if needed
- chopped kale: 2 big handfuls
- wild rice: 1 cup
- bay leaves: 2
- cooked turkey or chicken, shredded or chopped: 2–3 cups
- cream cheese, cut into small pieces (or ⅓ cup grated Parm): 4 oz (½ block)
- lemon, halved: 1
- toasted sourdough with butter and flaky salt (optional but recommended): to taste

### Method
1. Cook sausage in a large pot over medium-high heat, breaking into small pieces, until crispy and browned. Transfer to a bowl with a slotted spoon, leaving the grease in the pot.
2. Add butter to the pot. Once melted, add celery, carrots, onion, and mushrooms. Cook, stirring every minute, 10–15 minutes until all moisture has evaporated.
3. Stir in flour, salt, pepper, rosemary, smoked paprika, and Cajun seasoning. Cook 1 minute.
4. Add stock, kale, wild rice, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low, cover, and simmer 30–40 minutes until rice is tender.
5. Remove bay leaves. Stir in shredded turkey or chicken, cooked sausage, cream cheese (or Parm), and juice of ½ lemon. Simmer uncovered a few minutes until cream cheese has melted in.
6. Taste and adjust salt and lemon juice. Add up to 2 more cups stock if a thinner consistency is preferred.
7. Serve with toasted sourdough, good butter, and flaky salt.

### Notes

Serves 6–8 — leftover opportunity for a household of 3. Creamy or brothy depending on whether you add cream cheese/Parm.
