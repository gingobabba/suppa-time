### Ingredients
- medium yellow onion: 1
- large carrots, washed: 3
- extra-virgin olive oil: 2 tablespoons
- garlic cloves: 4
- low-sodium chicken broth or stock: 2 quarts (8 cups)
- boneless skinless chicken thighs: 1½ pounds
- orzo (or ¾ cup white rice): 1 cup
- cannellini beans, drained but not rinsed: 1 (14.5 oz) can
- kosher salt and pepper (white pepper if available): to taste
- large eggs: 4
- fresh lemon juice: ⅓ cup
- lemon, zested: 1
- grated Parmesan, plus more for garnish: ¼ cup
- kale, Swiss chard, escarole, or spinach, chopped: 1 bunch (4 cups)
- soft green herbs, chopped (dill preferred): 1 cup

### Method
1. Dice 1 medium yellow onion and 3 large carrots.
2. Warm 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add onion and carrot and cook for 4 minutes, stirring often.
3. Mince 4 garlic cloves. Add to pot and stir for 30 seconds.
4. Stir in 2 quarts chicken broth, 1½ pounds chicken thighs, 1 cup orzo, 1 can drained cannellini beans, ½ teaspoon kosher salt, and ¼ teaspoon pepper.
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook uncovered for 20 minutes.
6. Meanwhile, whisk together 4 eggs, ⅓ cup lemon juice, ¼ cup grated Parmesan, and zest of 1 lemon until fully combined with no streaks of white. Set aside.
7. De-rib and chop 1 bunch kale. Set aside.
8. After 20 minutes, transfer chicken to a cutting board. Turn off the stove and move pot to a cool burner for 5 minutes. Shred and roughly chop the chicken; leave on the board.
9. Ladle ½ cup broth into the egg mixture, whisking as you pour. Ladle in 1 more cup and whisk to combine (this is tempering — it prevents scrambled egg soup).
10. Stir the tempered egg mixture into the pot. Add the chopped kale and chicken.
11. Cook over low heat, stirring often, until soup has thickened and kale is tender.
12. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and lemon juice as needed.
13. Serve garnished with 1 cup chopped soft green herbs divided between 6 bowls, extra Parmesan, and optional lemon slices. As the soup sits it will thicken — stir in broth or water to loosen.

### Notes

Serves 6 — leftover opportunity for a household of 3. Eggs and lemon juice make a silky broth (avgolemono-style).
