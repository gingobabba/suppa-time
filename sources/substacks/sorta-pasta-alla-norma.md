### Ingredients
- globe eggplant (1 1/2 to 2 pound): 1
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1/4 cup plus 1 tablespoon
- kosher salt: to taste
- mild Italian sausage: 1 pound
- garlic cloves: 4
- pearl couscous or orzo pasta: 2 cups
- marinara sauce (28-ounce jar): 1 jar
- water: 2 1/2 cups
- Parmesan cheese (grated): 1/2 cup
- fresh basil leaves: 1 packed cup
- red pepper flakes: optional

### Method
1. Peel eggplant in stripes ("like a zebra"), then cut into ½-inch cubes. Warm ¼ cup olive oil in a wide skillet over medium-high heat. Add eggplant and 1 teaspoon salt and toss to coat. Cook, stirring every minute, 8–10 minutes until very tender and browned. Transfer to a bowl.
2. Return skillet to medium-high, warm remaining 1 tablespoon oil. Add sausage and cook, breaking into small crumbles, until almost cooked through. Add garlic and continue cooking until sausage is done.
3. Add cooked eggplant and pearl couscous. Stir for a few minutes to lightly toast the couscous. Stir in marinara sauce, water, and ¼ teaspoon salt.
4. Once the liquid reaches a boil, reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and cook 15–18 minutes until pasta is tender.
5. Stir in ¼ cup Parmesan and most of the basil (reserve some for garnish).
6. Serve topped with remaining Parmesan, basil, and red pepper flakes.
