### Ingredients
- large carrots, washed and chopped into bite-sized pieces: 3
- small onion (any color), minced: 1
- extra-virgin olive oil: 3 tablespoons
- kosher salt: to taste
- garlic cloves, thinly sliced or minced: 4
- ground turkey (or any ground meat): 1 pound
- small Yukon gold potato (4–6 oz), chopped into bite-sized pieces: 1
- low-sodium chicken stock (or water + Better Than Bouillon): 8 cups
- farro (10–20 min cook time): 1 cup
- green lentils, rinsed: 1 cup
- crushed tomatoes: 1 (28 oz) can
- Parmigiano-Reggiano block: 1
- harissa paste: 1–2 tablespoons
- lacinato kale, roughly chopped: 1 bunch (about 4 cups)
- lemon (preferably organic), zested: 1
- cream cheese (optional): 4 oz (half a block)

### Method
1. Warm olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add carrots, onion, and a big pinch of salt. Cook 3 minutes until slightly softened.
2. Add garlic, ground turkey, and another big pinch of salt. Cook, breaking turkey into small pieces, until cooked through.
3. Add potato, stock, farro, lentils, crushed tomatoes, the Parm rind, and harissa (start with 1 tablespoon; add more later for heat). Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook uncovered 20 minutes until farro is tender.
4. Meanwhile, roughly chop kale and zest and juice the lemon.
5. Remove and discard the Parm rind. Stir in kale, lemon zest, lemon juice, and cream cheese (if using). Stir until kale is wilted and cream cheese has melted in.
6. Adjust consistency: stir in more stock to thin, or keep simmering uncovered to thicken.
7. Serve topped with plenty of freshly grated Parm and a dollop of harissa.

### Notes

Serves 6 — leftover opportunity for a household of 3. Cream cheese is optional but makes it creamy.
