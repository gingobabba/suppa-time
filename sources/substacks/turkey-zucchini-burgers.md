### Ingredients

**Burgers:**
- zucchini (large): 1
- ground turkey: 1 pound
- lemon (large): 1
- panko or Italian breadcrumbs: 1/3 cup
- basil or mint, chopped: 1/4 cup
- large egg yolk: 1
- kosher salt: 1 teaspoon
- garlic powder: 1 teaspoon
- ground black pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
- mayonnaise: 1/2 cup
- miso: 1 tablespoon
- cooking spray
- English muffins: 4
- lettuce, tomato, onion or other toppings: to taste

**Watermelon and Feta Salad:**
- watermelon, cubed: 3 cups
- feta, crumbled: 1/2 cup
- basil or mint, chopped: 1/4 cup
- balsamic vinegar or lemon/lime juice: 1 tablespoon

### Method
1. Grate 2 cups of zucchini onto a thin dish towel. Squeeze out as much moisture as possible over a sink. Transfer to a large bowl. Slice any remaining zucchini into planks to grill alongside the burgers.
2. Add ground turkey, lemon zest, breadcrumbs, basil or mint, egg yolk, salt, garlic powder, and pepper to the bowl. Mix with hands until combined. Divide into 4 patties slightly larger than an English muffin.
3. Place patties on a parchment-lined baking sheet and freeze for 20 minutes (or refrigerate up to 48 hours). Freezing is essential for grilling — skip only if cooking in a skillet.
4. Make the miso mayo: stir together mayo, miso, and 1 tablespoon lemon juice until smooth. Can be made up to 1 week ahead.
5. Just before serving, toss together watermelon, feta, basil or mint, and vinegar or lemon juice for the salad.
6. Preheat outdoor grill to 500°F. Spray patties all over with cooking spray — this is essential for preventing sticking. Grill for 4 minutes per side, or until they reach 165°F internal temperature.
7. After flipping the burgers, spray the cut sides of the English muffins with cooking spray and grill cut-side down for about 1 minute until toasted.
8. Slather muffins with miso mayo, top with burgers and toppings of choice. Serve alongside the watermelon feta salad.

### Notes
Substitute: sub grilled zucchini planks (from the leftover zucchini) for a side instead of the watermelon salad if preferred.
