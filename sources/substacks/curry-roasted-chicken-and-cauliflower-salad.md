### Ingredients
- cauliflower head, cut into 1-inch flat florets: 1
- small red onion, thinly sliced: 1
- boneless skinless chicken thighs: 1 1/2 pounds
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1/4 cup
- kosher salt and black pepper: to taste
- smoked paprika: 1 teaspoon
- curry powder, divided: 2 teaspoons
- ground coriander: 1 teaspoon
- garlic powder: 1 teaspoon
- mayonnaise: 1/2 cup
- Greek yogurt: 1/2 cup
- lime juice: juice of 2 limes
- water: 2 tablespoons
- honey: 1 tablespoon
- small honeycrisp apple, cut into batons: 1
- dates, pitted and chopped (or 1/3 cup dried cranberries or golden raisins): 5
- arugula (5-oz container): 1
- Parmesan, shaved with a vegetable peeler: 4 ounces
- roasted salted pistachios, sunflower seeds, pepitas, or almonds: 1/4 cup

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. Cut cauliflower into 1-inch pieces and thinly slice the onion.
3. Toss cauliflower, onion, and chicken thighs on a large rimmed baking sheet with olive oil, 2 teaspoons salt, pepper, paprika, 1 teaspoon curry powder, coriander, and garlic powder until evenly coated.
4. Roast for 30 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and cauliflower is tender and golden. If the chicken is done but the cauliflower needs more colour, remove the chicken and broil the cauliflower on high for a few minutes, watching constantly.
5. Meanwhile, make the dressing: shake or stir together mayo, Greek yogurt, lime juice, water, honey, remaining 1 teaspoon curry powder, 1/4 teaspoon salt, and pepper until combined. Taste and adjust.
6. Cut apple into batons. Pit and chop dates.
7. Place arugula in a large shallow serving bowl and season with a pinch of salt. Chop the chicken and arrange on top with the roasted cauliflower and onions, shaved Parmesan, dates, and nuts. Dress to taste, starting with half the dressing and adding more as needed.
