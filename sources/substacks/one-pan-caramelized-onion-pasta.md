### Ingredients
- spaghetti, uncooked: 1 pound
- yellow onion, very thinly sliced: 1 pound (1 large or 2 small)
- garlic cloves, minced: 4
- extra-virgin olive oil: ¼ cup
- tomato paste: ¼ cup
- reduced-sodium Vegetable Better Than Bouillon: 2 tablespoons
- anchovy paste (or 4 diced anchovies): 1 tablespoon
- dried Italian seasoning: 2 teaspoons
- kosher salt: 1½ teaspoons
- red pepper flakes: ½ teaspoon
- hot water: 4¼ cups
- Boursin Garlic and Herbs (5.2 oz package): 1
- clear vinegar or lemon juice (e.g. champagne vinegar): 1 tablespoon
- freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano: ½ cup
- more grated Parm and red pepper flakes (optional, for serving): to taste

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, layer in this order: spaghetti on the bottom, then onions, garlic, olive oil, tomato paste, Better Than Bouillon, anchovy paste, Italian seasoning, salt, and red pepper flakes. Use your hands to toss the onions with the pastes, oil, and seasonings — leave the spaghetti on the bottom.
3. Pour hot water in along one corner of the dish to avoid washing the seasoning off the onions. Place the Boursin block on top. Cover tightly with foil or a baking sheet.
4. Bake 45 minutes. Uncover (do not stir) and bake another 10–15 minutes until onions are browned and soft.
5. Add vinegar and Parm and toss with tongs until combined and saucy. (Discard any noodles that stayed hard.) Taste and adjust: salt, red pepper flakes, vinegar, or a splash of hot water for more sauce.
6. Serve topped with extra Parm and red pepper flakes. (If the pasta absorbs the sauce as it sits, add a splash of hot water and toss to loosen.)

### Notes

80 min total but mostly hands-off. Serves 6–8 — flags as a leftover opportunity for a household of 3.
