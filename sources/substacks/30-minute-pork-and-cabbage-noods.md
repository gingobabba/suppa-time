### Ingredients
- kosher salt: to taste
- spaghetti: 1 pound
- green cabbage (half a head), core removed, thinly sliced (or 10-oz bag shredded cabbage)
- fresh ginger, grated (or 1/2 tsp ground ginger): 2-inch piece
- garlic cloves, grated or minced: 2
- fresh chives, thinly sliced: 1/2 oz (about 1/4 cup)
- ground pork: 1 pound
- low-sodium soy sauce: 1/4 cup
- unseasoned rice vinegar: 2 tablespoons
- toasted sesame oil, plus more for serving: 1 tablespoon
- hot sauce or chili paste (sriracha, gochujang, sambal oelek — optional): to taste
- toasted sesame seeds, plus more for serving: 3 tablespoons

### Method
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti per package instructions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining. Drain and shake dry.
2. Meanwhile, slice the cabbage as thinly as possible. Grate the ginger and garlic. Slice the chives.
3. In a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat, press ground pork into a thin layer. Cook undisturbed for 5–7 minutes until browned underneath. Break into small crumbles, then add cabbage, ginger, garlic, and a pinch of salt. Cook until cabbage is wilted, 2–4 minutes. Remove from heat until the noodles are ready.
4. Add cooked spaghetti, 1/2 cup pasta water, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil to the skillet. Add hot sauce if using. Set over medium heat and toss for 1–2 minutes until the pasta is coated in sauce. Add more pasta water as needed until the noodles are glossy and saucy.
5. Remove from heat and stir in chives and sesame seeds. Serve with an extra drizzle of sesame oil and more sesame seeds.
