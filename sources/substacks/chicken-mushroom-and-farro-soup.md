### Ingredients
- carrots, finely chopped: 4
- celery stalks, finely chopped: 3
- large leek, halved vertically and thinly sliced: 1
- mushrooms, thinly sliced: 12 oz
- garlic cloves, minced: 4
- olive oil: 2 tablespoons
- kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper: to taste
- boneless, skinless chicken thighs: 1½ pounds
- farro: 1¼ cups
- kale, tough stems removed: 1 bunch
- fresh rosemary sprigs (or 6 thyme sprigs): 2
- low-sodium chicken stock (or water + Better Than Bouillon): 2 quarts (8 cups)
- lemon, juiced: 1
- to make creamy (optional, pick one): grated Parmesan ⅓ cup, cream cheese 4 oz, heavy cream ½ cup, half and half ⅓ cup, or plain yogurt ⅓ cup

### Method
1. Add carrots, celery, leek, mushrooms, and garlic to a large cold pot. Drizzle with olive oil, add 1 teaspoon salt and several grinds of pepper, and cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, 7–10 minutes until vegetables are tender and liquid has evaporated. (If still liquidy once tender, increase to high heat and stir constantly until evaporated.)
2. Add chicken thighs, farro, rosemary sprigs, kale, and stock. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low, cover with lid slightly ajar, and cook 20 minutes.
3. Remove herb sprigs. Transfer chicken to a cutting board and shred with two forks. (If not shredding easily or not at 165°F, return to pot and cook a few minutes more.) Return shredded chicken to the pot.
4. Stir in juice of ½ lemon to start, adjusting up to the full lemon to taste. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Optional — to make creamy: stir in your chosen creamy addition and simmer a few minutes until incorporated and thickened. (It may look curdled at first — keep stirring.) Taste and adjust seasoning.
6. Serve topped with grated Parmesan, croutons, or a drizzle of chili crisp.

### Notes

Serves 6–8 — great leftover opportunity for a household of 3. Optional creamy additions listed above.
