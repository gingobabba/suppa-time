### Ingredients
- brussels sprouts: 1 pound
- gnocchi (uncooked, from the pasta aisle): 1 pound
- mushrooms (any kind), sliced 1/4-inch thick: 3–4 ounces
- garlic cloves: 2
- organic lemon: 1
- extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling: 1/4 cup plus 1 tablespoon
- kosher salt: 1 1/2 teaspoons
- black pepper: 1/4 teaspoon
- mild or spicy Italian sausage links: 1 pound
- burrata: 8 ounces
- red pepper flakes (optional): pinch
- store-bought pesto (optional): to taste

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. Trim any brown roots from the brussels sprouts. Cut through the root end into thin planks — about 5–6 per sprout.
3. Place brussels sprouts, mushrooms, and gnocchi (straight from the package, no pre-cooking needed) on your largest rimmed baking sheet. Grate the garlic and lemon zest directly over the top. Add olive oil, salt, and pepper and toss to coat. Spread into an even layer.
4. Squeeze the sausage out of the casing in 1/2-inch increments directly onto the sheet pan to create small meatballs — about 5 per link. They don't need to be perfectly round. Spread everything out as much as possible.
5. Take the burrata out of the fridge to come to room temperature.
6. Roast for 25–30 minutes until crispy on the outside and tender inside. Broil for a minute at the end if the top needs more colour — watch closely.
7. Divide between four bowls. Top each with half a ball of burrata, a drizzle of olive oil, and red pepper flakes or pesto if using.
