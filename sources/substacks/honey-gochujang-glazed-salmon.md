### Ingredients
- white rice (sushi rice preferred), rinsed: 1 1/2 cups
- coconut milk (14-oz can): 1
- water, divided: 1 1/4 cups plus 1 tablespoon
- honey, divided: 5 tablespoons
- kosher salt: to taste
- broccoli florets, cut into 1 1/2-inch pieces: 1 pound
- low-sodium soy sauce: 1/4 cup
- fish sauce: 1 tablespoon
- gochujang or sriracha: 1 tablespoon
- garlic powder: 1/2 teaspoon
- salmon filets (6-oz each), cut into 1-inch cubes: 4
- cooking oil: about 2 tablespoons
- unsalted butter: 2 tablespoons

### Method
1. Combine rice, coconut milk, 1 1/4 cups water, 2 tablespoons honey, and 1/2 teaspoon salt in a wide saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce to the lowest heat possible. Cover and cook for 10 minutes.
2. Poke holes all over a large sheet of parchment paper. After 10 minutes, stir the rice, lay the parchment on top, and add the broccoli florets on top of the parchment. Cover and cook on low for 10 more minutes until broccoli is tender and rice is cooked through. Keep covered until serving.
3. While the rice cooks, whisk together soy sauce, remaining 3 tablespoons honey, fish sauce, gochujang, 1 tablespoon water, and garlic powder in a bowl.
4. Season salmon cubes with a small pinch of salt. Warm enough oil to coat a large skillet (preferably non-stick) over medium-high heat. Add salmon in an even layer and cook for 3–4 minutes until crisp on the bottom. Use a thin spatula to flip without tearing, then cook for another 3 minutes until the salmon releases easily.
5. Add the sauce and butter to the skillet. Increase heat to high and cook for 2–3 minutes, gently turning the salmon to coat, until the sauce thickens and the edges are golden. Watch closely — the sauce should thicken to a glaze, not evaporate.
6. Serve bowls of coconut rice and broccoli topped with salmon, drizzled with the pan sauce.
