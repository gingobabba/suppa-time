### Ingredients
- yellow onion (medium): 1, finely chopped
- red bell pepper (large): 1, finely chopped
- garlic cloves: 4, minced
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1 tablespoon
- ground beef: 1 pound
- cauliflower rice (frozen or fresh): 1 pound
- taco seasoning: 1 tablespoon plus 2 teaspoons
- kosher salt: 1 teaspoon
- spinach or kale: 2 big handfuls
- all-purpose flour: 1 tablespoon
- diced tomatoes (28-ounce can): 1 can
- corn tortillas (6- to 8-inch): 6
- cheddar or Mexican cheese (grated/shredded): 1 packed cup

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Warm a 12-inch ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat. Add olive oil, onion, bell pepper, garlic, ground beef, cauliflower rice, taco seasoning, and salt. Cook 5–6 minutes, breaking beef into small crumbles, until beef is cooked through and vegetables are tender.
3. Taste and adjust seasoning. Stir in flour until fully absorbed, then stir in diced tomatoes and chopped greens. Cook until greens wilt and mixture thickens. (If there's excess liquid, increase heat and stir until evaporated.) Turn off heat.
4. Char tortillas directly over a gas flame, or toast in a dry skillet over medium-high heat.
5. Press tortillas into the beef mixture, layering imperfectly with some filling between each. Top with grated cheese.
6. Bake 10–12 minutes until cheese is browned and mixture is bubbling. Let cool slightly before scooping onto plates.
7. Serve with sour cream, pickled red onions, salsa, hot sauce, lime wedges, and/or cilantro.
