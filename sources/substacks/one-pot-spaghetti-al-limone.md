### Ingredients
- dry spaghetti: 1 pound
- lemons (preferably organic), zested and juiced: 2
- garlic cloves, peeled and grated: 2
- unsalted butter: 6 tablespoons
- kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper: to taste
- water: 4½ cups
- Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated: 3 oz (2–3 cups)

### Method
1. Add spaghetti to a large wide saucepan. Grate in the lemon zest and garlic. Add butter, 1 teaspoon salt, and several grinds of pepper. Pour in water.
2. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-high. Use tongs to turn the pasta every 30 seconds until al dente and most (but not all) of the water has evaporated, 7–9 minutes. (If water evaporates before pasta is done, add a splash more.)
3. Stir in ¼ cup lemon juice. Remove from heat and let cool for 3 minutes. (Do not skip — the cheese will clump if added while too hot.)
4. A little at a time, add the finely grated Pecorino, tossing vigorously with tongs after each addition before adding more. Keep stirring until saucy and combined. (For a creamier result, add more butter or a splash of water.)
5. Taste and adjust with more lemon juice, salt, or cheese.
6. Serve immediately, topped with extra Pecorino and black pepper.

### Notes

One-pot, 20-minute pasta. Everything cooks together in a single pot — minimal cleanup. Serves 4 (possibly up to 6).
