### Ingredients
- olive oil, divided: 4 tablespoons
- lemon (zest and juice), divided: 1
- garlic cloves, grated: 2
- kosher salt and pepper: to taste
- dried oregano (or 2 teaspoons fresh): 1 teaspoon
- bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs: 2 pounds
- fingerling potatoes, halved (or quartered if large): 1 1/2 pounds
- fennel head, thinly sliced: 1
- feta cheese, crumbled: 2–4 ounces
- fresh dill, chopped: 3 tablespoons

### Method
1. Whisk 2 tablespoons olive oil, zest and juice of half the lemon, grated garlic, 1 teaspoon salt, 1/4 teaspoon pepper, and oregano in a large bowl. Add chicken thighs and toss to coat. Marinate in the refrigerator for up to 12 hours, or proceed immediately.
2. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment.
3. Toss potatoes and fennel with remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil, 3/4 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper on the baking sheet. Spread into an even layer. Roast for 15 minutes, then stir.
4. Shake excess marinade off the chicken. Nestle skin-side up between the potatoes and fennel. Pat the skin dry with a paper towel to ensure crispiness.
5. Roast for 30–35 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and potatoes and fennel are browned and tender.
6. Finish with juice of the remaining lemon half, crumbled feta, and fresh dill. Season to taste and serve immediately.

### Notes
Fennel: see source link for prep notes. Can substitute with thinly sliced onion or leek if unavailable.
