### Ingredients
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1 tablespoon
- large egg: 1
- ground chicken: 1 pound
- Parmesan cheese, grated, divided: 1 cup
- fresh rosemary, finely minced: 1 tablespoon
- breadcrumbs or almond flour: 1/3 cup
- lemon (preferably Meyer), zested and juiced, divided: 1
- kosher salt: to taste
- red pepper flakes, divided: 1/2 teaspoon
- large shallot, minced: 1
- garlic cloves, minced: 2
- unsalted butter: 3 tablespoons
- orzo pasta: 1 cup
- low-sodium chicken stock: 2–3 cups
- frozen broccoli florets: 1 cup

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Grease a 12-inch oven-proof skillet with olive oil.
2. Whisk the egg in a large bowl. Add ground chicken, 1/2 cup Parmesan, rosemary (mince very finely — large pieces are unpleasant in a bite), breadcrumbs, lemon zest, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes. Mix with a fork or hands until just combined.
3. Grease a tablespoon measure with oil. Scoop heaping spoonfuls (about 2 tablespoons each) into the skillet — you should get 14–18 meatballs. Grease your hands and roll into balls.
4. Bake for 18 minutes. Meanwhile, mince the shallot and garlic, keeping them separate. Halve the zested lemon.
5. Transfer cooked meatballs to a plate. Place the skillet over medium heat and melt butter. Place the lemon halves cut-side down in the skillet to char — this deepens the flavour.
6. Add shallot and a pinch of salt to the other side of the skillet and cook for 5–7 minutes until tender. Stir in garlic, orzo, and remaining 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes and cook for 1 minute. Remove the lemon halves. Stir in 2 cups stock and bring to a boil. Reduce to medium-low and cook, stirring often, for 8–10 minutes until orzo is al dente.
7. Stir in more stock as needed to keep it loose and saucy. Add the frozen broccoli, remaining 1/2 cup Parmesan, and juice from the charred lemon.
8. Turn off the heat, nestle the meatballs on top, and cover for a few minutes to heat through. Garnish with Parmesan and red pepper flakes.

### Notes
Peas substituted with frozen broccoli.
