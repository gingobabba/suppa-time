### Ingredients
- unsalted butter: 1 stick (8 tablespoons)
- all-purpose flour: ¾ cup
- large yellow onion, diced: 1
- large celery ribs, diced: 3
- large red bell pepper, diced: 1
- garlic cloves, minced: 4
- boneless, skinless chicken thighs (raw), trimmed and halved: 1½–2 pounds
- Cajun seasoning: 1 tablespoon
- smoked paprika: 1 teaspoon
- tomato paste: 1 tablespoon
- low-sodium chicken stock (or water + Better Than Bouillon): 2 quarts (8 cups)
- tomato sauce: 1 (14 oz) can
- bay leaves: 2
- fresh okra, sliced ¼-inch thick (or frozen pre-sliced): 1 pound
- andouille sausage links, thinly sliced: 1 pound
- kosher salt and pepper: to taste

### Method
1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Whisk in flour and reduce heat to medium-low. Cook the roux, stirring every 1–2 minutes, for about 30 minutes until it reaches a rich peanut-butter brown. (For deeper flavor, continue 10–15 more minutes until milk-chocolate brown. Do not let it burn.)
2. While the roux cooks, dice onion, celery, and bell pepper; mince garlic; trim and halve chicken thighs; slice okra and sausage.
3. Once the roux is dark brown, stir in onion, celery, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook 5 minutes, stirring. Stir in Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, and tomato paste and cook 1 more minute.
4. Stir in stock and tomato sauce. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium. Add chicken thighs and bay leaves and cook uncovered for 15 minutes.
5. Add okra and sausage and cook 15 more minutes until okra is tender. (The gumbo will look slimy shortly after the okra goes in — this is normal and will resolve.)
6. Remove chicken thighs and shred with two forks. Stir back in. Discard bay leaves.
7. Taste and season with salt and pepper in small increments until the flavors pop.
8. Serve over white rice with hot sauce (Crystal preferred) and sliced scallions.

### Notes

Serves 6–8 — good leftover opportunity for a household of 3. Serve over rice.
