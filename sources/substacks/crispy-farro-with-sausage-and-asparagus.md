### Ingredients
- pearled farro, uncooked (or 3 cups pre-cooked): 1 1/2 cups
- kosher salt: to taste
- mild or hot Italian sausage, casings removed: 1 pound
- scallions (1 bunch), trimmed and thinly sliced on a diagonal, dark green parts kept separate
- asparagus (1 bunch), trimmed and thinly sliced on a diagonal into 1/2-inch pieces
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1 tablespoon, plus more as needed
- red wine vinegar: 1 tablespoon, plus more to taste

### Method
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add farro and simmer for 15–20 minutes until al dente — tender but still slightly chewy. Drain, rinse under cold water, and shake thoroughly dry.
2. While farro cooks, remove sausage casings, slice scallions (keeping dark green tops separate), and slice asparagus.
3. Wipe out the pot (or use a large skillet). Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil over medium-high. Add sausage and cook, breaking into small pieces, for 8–10 minutes until crispy. Transfer to a plate with a slotted spoon, leaving the fat in the pot.
4. Add farro and scallion whites to the sausage fat. Spread flat and cook undisturbed until golden on the bottom, 3–5 minutes — listen for the sizzle to turn to a crackle. Add a splash of olive oil if anything starts to burn.
5. Add asparagus and a big pinch of salt. Stir, spread flat again, and cook until the bottom is golden once more, 3–5 minutes.
6. Return the sausage and any collected juices to the pot. Stir for 1–2 minutes until the asparagus is crisp-tender. Turn off the heat and stir in the scallion greens and red wine vinegar.
7. Season to taste with salt and more vinegar, adding a small splash at a time, until punchy and well-balanced.

### Notes
If you can't find pearled farro, cooking time will be longer — see source link for guidance.
