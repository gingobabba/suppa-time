### Ingredients
- garlic cloves, grated, divided: 4
- lemon, zested and juiced: 1
- peeled, deveined shrimp (~20/pound): 1 pound
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1 tablespoon
- kosher salt: to taste
- unsalted butter: 2 tablespoons
- large shallot, minced (about 1/2 cup): 1
- red pepper flakes (omit or reduce for spice-averse): 1/2 teaspoon
- low-sodium chicken stock, plus more as needed: 3 to 3 1/2 cups
- orzo pasta: 1 1/2 cups
- frozen broccoli florets: 1 1/2 cups
- Parmesan cheese, grated, plus more for garnish: 2 ounces (1/2 cup)
- fresh basil, finely chopped, plus more for garnish: 1/2 cup

### Method
1. Combine shrimp, half the grated garlic, all the lemon zest, 1 tablespoon olive oil, and 3/4 teaspoon salt in a bowl. Set aside to marinate at room temperature.
2. Melt butter in a 12-inch skillet or braiser over medium heat. Cook the shallot, remaining garlic, and red pepper flakes until softened, about 4 minutes. Stir in 3 cups chicken stock and orzo and bring to a boil. Cover, reduce to low, and cook for 8 minutes.
3. Remove the lid and lay the marinated shrimp in an even layer over the orzo. Cover and cook for 5 minutes, until most shrimp look cooked through (some translucency is fine — they'll finish in the next step).
4. Stir in the frozen broccoli, Parmesan, lemon juice, and basil. Add more stock if the skillet looks dry. Stir until all shrimp are opaque and curled into a C-shape and the orzo is a loose, risotto-like consistency. Add stock a splash at a time to loosen if needed.
5. Serve garnished with extra basil, red pepper flakes, and Parmesan.

### Notes
Peas substituted with frozen broccoli.
