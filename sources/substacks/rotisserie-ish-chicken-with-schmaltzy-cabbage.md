### Ingredients
- green cabbage head (~2 lbs), cored and thinly sliced: 1
- extra-virgin olive oil: as needed
- kosher salt: to taste
- brown sugar: 1 tablespoon
- smoked paprika: 1 tablespoon
- chili powder: 1 teaspoon
- ground black pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
- whole chicken (3 1/2 to 4 pounds): 1
- medium sweet potatoes: 4
- apple cider vinegar or lemon juice: 1 tablespoon
- unsalted butter: 4 tablespoons

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. Core and thinly slice the cabbage into 1/4-inch pieces. Toss on your largest rimmed baking sheet with 3 tablespoons olive oil and 1 1/2 teaspoons salt.
3. Stir together 2 tablespoons olive oil, 1 tablespoon salt, brown sugar, paprika, chili powder, and pepper in a small bowl.
4. Pat the chicken completely dry. Place breast-side up and coat all over with the spice paste. Tuck the wings under the bird to prevent burning. Tie the legs together with kitchen twine (or a rolled strip of foil).
5. Prick the sweet potatoes all over with a fork and rub with a thin coat of olive oil. Place on the cabbage or on a separate sheet pan above the chicken.
6. Roast on the centre rack for 45–60 minutes, stirring the cabbage every 20 minutes, until a thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the leg reads 160°F. If the skin darkens too early, cover the chicken loosely with foil. Start checking at 45 minutes.
7. Remove from the oven. Tilt the chicken over the sheet pan using tongs through the cavity to drain the internal juices over the cabbage. Transfer the chicken to a cutting board to rest.
8. Return cabbage and sweet potatoes to the oven for 10 minutes until the cabbage edges are brown and frizzled. Broil for a minute if needed, watching closely.
9. Stir vinegar into the cabbage and season to taste. Halve the sweet potatoes and mash 1 tablespoon butter and a pinch of salt and pepper into each.
10. Carve the chicken and serve with the cabbage and a sweet potato.

### Notes
Whole chicken time varies by bird size and oven — start checking at 45 minutes.
