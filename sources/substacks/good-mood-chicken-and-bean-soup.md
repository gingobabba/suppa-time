### Ingredients
- chickpeas, drained and rinsed: 1 (15 oz) can
- cannellini beans, drained and rinsed: 1 (15 oz) can
- kidney beans, drained and rinsed: 1 (15 oz) can
- kale, finely chopped (or spinach): 4 cups
- garlic cloves, minced: 6
- boneless, skinless chicken (breasts, tenders, or thighs): 1½ pounds
- red pepper flakes: 1 teaspoon
- oil-packed julienne-cut sun-dried tomatoes, drained: 1 (8 oz) jar
- low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth (or water + Better Than Bouillon): 6 cups
- dried Italian seasoning or oregano: 1 teaspoon
- kosher salt: 1 teaspoon
- black pepper: ½ teaspoon
- heavy cream: 1 cup
- grated Parmesan, plus more for serving: ½ cup

### Method
1. Add beans, kale, garlic, chicken, red pepper flakes, sun-dried tomatoes, broth, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper to a large heavy-bottomed pot. (If you have a Parm rind, add it now for extra flavor — remove before serving.) If the kale isn't fully submerged, it will wilt down quickly; add more broth if the chicken and beans are still not covered.
2. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low and cook uncovered for 20 minutes.
3. Shred the chicken directly in the pot using two forks. Lightly smash some of the beans with the back of a spoon.
4. Stir in heavy cream and Parmesan until well combined. Taste and adjust salt as needed.
5. Serve topped with a generous layer of grated Parmesan.

### Notes

40 min, mostly hands-off. Serves 4–6 — leftover opportunity for a household of 3.
