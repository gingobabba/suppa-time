### Ingredients
- olive oil, divided: 3 tablespoons
- large yellow onion, thinly sliced into half-moons: 1
- green bell peppers, cored, seeded, and thinly sliced: 2 (about 2 cups)
- white mushrooms, thinly sliced: 10 oz (about 3 cups)
- garlic cloves, minced: 4
- shaved Angus steak: 14 oz
- tapioca flour or arrowroot starch (can sub cornstarch): 2 teaspoons
- kosher salt: ½ teaspoon
- ground black pepper: ½ teaspoon
- coconut aminos (or reduced-sodium tamari or soy sauce): ¼ cup
- mozzarella, shredded: ~6 oz (1 cup)

### Method
1. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Add onion and bell peppers and cook, stirring occasionally, until onion is caramelized and peppers are soft, 25–30 minutes. (Reduce heat to medium-low if browning too quickly; add a splash of water if needed.)
2. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add mushrooms and garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, until mushrooms are softened, 5–7 minutes.
3. Create a well in the center of the pan and add remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil. Add shaved steak and cook, stirring frequently, until cooked through and browned, about 2 minutes.
4. Add tapioca flour, salt, and pepper and mix until well combined. Stir in coconut aminos and reduce heat to low. Sprinkle mozzarella over the top, cover (use a sheet pan if the skillet has no lid), and cook until sauce is thickened and cheese is melted, about 5 minutes.
5. Divide among plates and serve.

### Notes

Deconstructed Philly cheesesteak. Avocado oil subbed with olive oil. Serve straight from the skillet or in a hoagie roll.
