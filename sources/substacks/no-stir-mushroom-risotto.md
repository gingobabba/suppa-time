### Ingredients

**Risotto:**
- medium yellow onion, thinly sliced: 1
- mushrooms (any kind), sliced: 1 pound
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1/4 cup
- kosher salt and black pepper: to taste
- short grain brown rice: 1 1/2 cups
- beef stock (or water + beef-flavored Better Than Bouillon), divided: 6 1/2 to 7 cups
- white wine (not sweet; or 1/4 cup red): 1/2 cup
- lemon juice: juice of 1/2 lemon
- Gruyère, grated: 6 ounces (1 1/2 cups)
- Parmigiano Reggiano, grated, plus more for garnish: 2 ounces (1/4 cup)

**Walnut Chili Crunch:**
- extra-virgin olive oil: 2 tablespoons
- raw walnuts (or pecans, hazelnuts, or any nut): 1 cup
- lemon zest: zest of 1 lemon
- red pepper flakes: 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon
- fresh parsley, finely chopped: 1/4 cup

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 350°F with a rack in the middle.
2. Warm olive oil in a large oven-proof pot over high heat. Add onions and mushrooms and toss to coat. Let sit for 3 minutes, stir, then let sit for another 3 minutes — high heat helps moisture evaporate and browns them quickly. Season with 1 teaspoon salt and 1/4 teaspoon pepper and continue stirring until very tender and browned.
3. Stir in the brown rice and cook for 1 minute to toast the grains. Pour in 6 cups stock and bring to a boil.
4. Cover the pot (a baking sheet works if there's no lid) and transfer to the oven. Bake for 50–60 minutes until all liquid is absorbed.
5. While the risotto bakes, make the Walnut Chili Crunch: warm olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Crush walnuts in your fist as you add them. Toast with a pinch of salt for 6–8 minutes until golden. Add lemon zest and red pepper flakes and stir for 30 seconds — no longer or they'll burn. Turn off heat, stir in parsley, and season to taste.
6. Remove the pot from the oven and place over low heat. Stir in the white wine, remaining 1/4 cup stock, lemon juice, Gruyère, and Parmesan until the cheese is melted and the risotto is loose and saucy. Add more stock if needed — it should not be thick and gloopy.
7. Taste and adjust with more lemon, Parmesan, or salt as needed.
8. Serve in bowls topped with Parmesan and a generous sprinkle of Walnut Chili Crunch.
