### Ingredients
- Parmigiano Reggiano block: 8 ounces
- garlic cloves, peeled and roughly chopped (buy pre-peeled): 20
- high-quality extra-virgin olive oil: 1/2 cup
- kosher salt: to taste
- lacinato kale (about 1 pound), ends trimmed: 1 bunch
- spinach (about 12 oz), ends trimmed (or 2 bags baby spinach): 1 bunch
- pasta (bucatini or any long thick noodle): 1 pound
- reserved pasta cooking water: 1 cup
- lemons: 2
- red pepper flakes (optional): for garnish

### Method
1. Cut the rind off the Parmesan, cut into chunks, and blend until finely grated. Leave in the blender for the sauce.
2. Add garlic and olive oil to a small pot. Cook over low heat for 5–7 minutes until garlic is light golden and softened. Remove from heat and let cool.
3. Bring a large pot of heavily salted water (about 2 tablespoons salt) to a boil. Add kale and cook for 2 minutes. Add spinach and cook for 2–3 more minutes until both are wilted. Remove from heat.
4. Use tongs to transfer the greens to the blender, squeezing out excess water as you go — a little water is fine. Do NOT drain the pot. Return the pot to high heat and bring back to a boil.
5. Cook pasta per package instructions. Reserve 1 cup of cooking water, then drain the pasta and return it to the pot.
6. Make the sauce: add the cooled garlic and all its oil to the blender with the zest and juice of 1 lemon and 1 1/2 teaspoons salt. Blend on high until smooth. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time until the sauce is the consistency of a pourable marinara — thick but drizzleable. Taste and adjust with more salt or lemon juice.
7. Pour 1 cup of sauce into the pasta and toss. Add more sauce until the pasta is glossy and well-coated — about 1 1/2 cups total. Stir in more pasta water if needed to thin it out.
8. Serve garnished with more Parmesan and red pepper flakes.

### Notes
Save the Parmesan rind — it makes a great addition to brothy soups like Lemony Chicken Orzo.
