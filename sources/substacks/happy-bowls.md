### Ingredients

**Cashew sauce:**
- roasted cashews (sub: 1 can cannellini beans + 1 tbsp olive oil): 2 cups
- fresh soft green herbs (basil, cilantro, or dill): 1 cup
- apple cider vinegar: 2 tablespoons
- hot water, plus more as needed: 1/2 cup
- small garlic clove (or 1/8 tsp garlic powder): 1
- kosher salt: 1 teaspoon

**Roasted veg:**
- extra large sweet potato, 1-inch cubed: 1
- large beet, 1-inch cubed: 1
- head of cauliflower, cut into thin florets: 1
- small red onion, thinly sliced: 1
- extra-virgin olive oil: to coat
- kosher salt and black pepper: to taste
- garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika: a few shakes each

**Base and extras:**
- cooked grain (farro, rice, or similar — about 1 to 1 1/2 cups dry): as needed
- fresh spinach or other greens, chopped: a few handfuls
- optional toppings: crumbled feta, hard-boiled eggs, pickled red onions, roasted nuts or seeds, extra fresh herbs

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line two baking sheets with parchment.
2. Add cashews to a blender and cover with hot water. Set aside to soak while you prep the veg.
3. Scrub and cube the sweet potato and beet into 1-inch pieces. Place on separate halves of one baking sheet.
4. Cut the cauliflower into thin florets: halve through the stem, lay cut-side down, and slice into 1/2-inch planks, then cut apart florets. Place on one side of the second baking sheet. Add the sliced onion to the other side.
5. Drizzle all veg with olive oil and season each section with a big pinch of salt, a pinch of pepper, and shakes of garlic powder, onion powder, and smoked paprika. Toss each section to coat, keeping them separate.
6. Roast for 30–40 minutes until golden and fork-tender. Beets will be firmer than sweet potatoes — that's fine.
7. Meanwhile, cook your grains per package instructions and wash and chop your greens.
8. Make the cashew sauce: drain the soaking water from the blender. Add herbs, apple cider vinegar, 1/2 cup hot water, garlic, and salt. Blend until completely smooth, adding more hot water 1 tablespoon at a time until the sauce is thick but drizzleable. Taste and adjust salt.
9. Assemble bowls: add grains and greens to the base, top with roasted veg, drizzle generously with cashew sauce, and finish with any toppings.

### Notes
Cashew allergy sub: replace cashews with 1 can cannellini beans + 1 tbsp olive oil in the sauce.
