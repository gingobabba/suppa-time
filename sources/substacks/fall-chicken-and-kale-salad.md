### Ingredients
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1/4 cup
- balsamic vinegar: 1/4 cup
- kosher salt and black pepper: to taste
- garlic powder (or 1 small garlic clove, grated): 1/4 teaspoon
- honey or maple syrup (optional, if dressing is too tangy): 1 teaspoon
- curly or lacinato kale, tough stems removed, finely chopped: 1 bunch (4 heaping cups)
- cooked chicken breast, finely chopped (rotisserie works well): 1 large (about 1 1/2 cups)
- honeycrisp apple, cut into thin batons: 1 medium
- salted roasted almonds, roughly chopped: 1/3 cup
- white cheddar cheese, roughly chopped: 4 ounces
- dates, pitted and finely chopped (or 1/4 cup raisins, dried cranberries, or dried cherries): 3

### Method
1. Whisk olive oil, balsamic vinegar, salt, several grinds of pepper, and garlic powder in a large serving bowl. Taste and add honey or maple syrup if too tangy.
2. Add the chopped kale and a big pinch of salt. Massage the dressing into the kale until it softens — about 7 squeezes between your palms. Don't worry if it looks overdressed; the chicken will absorb the excess.
3. Add the chicken, apple, almonds, cheddar, and dates. Toss to coat. Let sit for 10 minutes if time allows before serving.

### Notes
Rotisserie chicken makes this weeknight-fast. Serves 3 exactly — no leftovers for a household of 3.
