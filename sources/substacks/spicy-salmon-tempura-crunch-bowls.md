### Ingredients

**Buttery brown rice:**
- brown rice (or frozen rice): 1 1/2 cups
- unsalted butter: 2 tablespoons
- kosher salt: to taste

**Salmon:**
- salmon, skin-on or off: 1 1/2 pounds
- kosher salt: 1 teaspoon
- garlic powder: 1/2 teaspoon
- ginger powder: 1/2 teaspoon
- soy sauce: 1 tablespoon
- sriracha (1 tbsp for flavor, 2 for heat): 1–2 tablespoons
- sesame oil, divided: 2 tablespoons
- maple syrup or brown sugar: 1 tablespoon
- unsalted butter: 2 tablespoons
- panko breadcrumbs: 1/4 cup
- sesame seeds: 3 tablespoons

**Greens:**
- cilantro (1 bunch), divided
- mixed greens (5-oz box): 1
- English cucumber, halved and thinly sliced into half moons: 1/2
- lime juice: juice of 1 large lime
- sesame oil: 2 tablespoons
- optional: thinly sliced apple, fennel, red onion, scallions, or peanuts

**Sriracha mayo:**
- mayonnaise: 1/2 cup
- sriracha: 1–2 tablespoons
- lime juice: juice of 1/2 lime
- kosher salt: pinch

### Method
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add brown rice and boil like pasta for about 40 minutes until tender. Drain, return to pot, stir in butter and a big pinch of salt. Cover until serving.
2. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.
3. Season salmon with salt, garlic powder, and ginger powder. Rub with soy sauce, sriracha, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, and maple syrup. Place skin-side down on the baking sheet.
4. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a bowl. Stir in remaining 1 tablespoon sesame oil, panko, sesame seeds, and a pinch of salt. Chop the entire bunch of cilantro. Finely dice 1/3 cup and stir into the panko; set the rest aside for the salad. Spread the breadcrumb mixture over the salmon.
5. Bake for 15–18 minutes until the salmon flakes easily when prodded with a fork.
6. Combine mixed greens, remaining cilantro, and cucumber in a large bowl. Dress with lime juice, sesame oil, and a big pinch of salt. Toss and adjust seasoning to taste.
7. Make the sriracha mayo: stir together mayo, sriracha, lime juice, and a pinch of salt. Adjust heat to taste.
8. Serve bowl-style with buttery rice on one side, dressed greens on the other, and salmon on top, drizzled with sriracha mayo. Add optional toppings as desired.
