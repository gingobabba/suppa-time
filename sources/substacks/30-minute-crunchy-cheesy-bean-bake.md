### Ingredients
- medium yellow onion, finely chopped: 1/2
- garlic cloves, minced: 2
- extra-virgin olive oil, divided: 6 tablespoons
- dried red pepper flakes: pinch
- kosher salt and black pepper: to taste
- tomato paste: 1 heaping tablespoon
- diced tomatoes (14.5-oz can): 1
- dried oregano: 1 tablespoon
- water: 1/4 cup
- cooked white beans (gigantes or cannellini), or 2 (15-oz) cans, rinsed and drained: 3 cups
- panko breadcrumbs: 1 cup
- Parmesan cheese, grated: 1/4 cup
- fresh thyme sprigs: 3 (leaves only)
- fresh mozzarella, thinly sliced: 5 slices (about 3 oz)
- for serving: green salad and/or crusty bread (optional)

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a large deep oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, red pepper flakes, a large pinch of salt, and a few grinds of pepper. Cook until the onion is soft, about 3 minutes.
3. Add tomato paste and cook, smashing it with a wooden spoon, until it turns a deeper colour, about 1 minute. Stir in diced tomatoes, oregano, and water. Increase heat to medium-high and cook for 5 minutes until warmed through. Add the beans and cook, stirring occasionally, for 4–5 minutes until heated through.
4. Mix panko, Parmesan, thyme leaves, and remaining 3 tablespoons olive oil in a small bowl until every crumb is coated in oil.
5. Taste the bean mixture and adjust salt as needed — add a small pinch at a time until the flavours pop.
6. Arrange mozzarella slices over the beans, then scatter the panko mixture evenly around the slices. Bake for 15 minutes until bubbly and golden. Broil briefly at the end if more browning is needed.
7. Serve with a green salad and/or crusty bread.
