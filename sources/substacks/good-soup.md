### Ingredients
- large carrots: 3
- large fennel bulb: 1
- large yellow onion, peeled and quartered: 1
- garlic cloves: 4
- mild Italian sausage: 1 pound
- extra-virgin olive oil: as needed
- kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper: to taste
- mild harissa paste/sauce: 1–2 tablespoons
- tomato paste: 2 tablespoons
- ground cumin: ¾ teaspoon
- lemon, washed and zested: 1
- water: 12 cups
- low-sodium chicken-flavored Better Than Bouillon: 3 tablespoons
- French green lentils, rinsed: 1 cup
- barley (or farro): 1 cup
- large bunch collards or lacinato kale: 1
- Parmigiano-Reggiano block: 1
- dried bay leaves or fresh rosemary sprigs: 2
- ½-inch cubed bread: 4 cups
- garlic powder: ¼ teaspoon

### Method
1. Pulse carrots, fennel, onion, and garlic in a food processor until very finely chopped. (Alternatively, finely chop by hand or blend one vegetable at a time in a blender.)
2. Cook sausage in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat, breaking into ½-inch pieces, until golden-brown all over, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a plate with a slotted spoon, leaving the fat in the pot.
3. Add 3 tablespoons olive oil to the pot and stir in the chopped vegetables. Season with about 1 teaspoon salt and ½ teaspoon pepper. Cook stirring often for 5 minutes, then partially cover and reduce to medium-low for 15 more minutes. (Vegetables should soften but not brown — lower heat if needed.) Meanwhile, roughly chop the collards.
4. Stir in harissa, tomato paste, cumin, and lemon zest. Cook 1–2 minutes until the paste darkens to a burnt-red colour.
5. Add water, Better Than Bouillon, lentils, barley, collards, Parm rind, bay leaves, and 1½ teaspoons salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low, cover fully, and cook for 45 minutes.
6. While the soup cooks, make croutons: coat the bottom of a large skillet generously with olive oil over medium-high heat. Add bread cubes and toss to coat, adding more oil as needed. Spread in an even layer, season with salt and garlic powder. Toast undisturbed until golden on the first side, 3–5 minutes, then stir and toast until golden all over. Set aside to cool.
7. Stir cooked sausage and lemon juice into the soup and heat through, about 3 minutes. Remove and discard the Parm rind and bay leaves.
8. Ladle into bowls and top with croutons and a generous amount of grated Parmigiano-Reggiano.

### Notes

90 min total, ~20 min active. Serves 6–8 — great leftover opportunity for a household of 3.
