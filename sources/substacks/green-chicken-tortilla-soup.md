### Ingredients

**Tortilla strips (optional — store-bought chips work just as well):**
- corn tortillas, cut into 1/4-inch slices: 8
- cooking oil: 2 tablespoons
- kosher salt: 3/4 teaspoon
- chili powder: 3/4 teaspoon
- garlic powder: 1/4 teaspoon

**Soup:**
- low-sodium chicken stock: 48 oz (6 cups)
- salsa verde or tomatillo salsa (jar): 1 (16- or 24-oz)
- green chiles, not drained: 1 (4-oz) can
- white beans (cannellini, navy, great Northern, or chickpeas), drained but not rinsed: 2 (15-oz) cans
- boneless skinless chicken thighs (raw): 2 pounds
- zucchini, diced: 1
- ground cumin: 1 1/2 teaspoons
- chili powder: 1 teaspoon
- smoked paprika: 1 teaspoon
- frozen white corn (or 2 cans, drained): 1 (16-oz) bag
- kale, chopped (stems removed): 3 cups
- cilantro (leaves and tender stems), finely chopped: 1 cup
- kosher salt: to taste

**Toppings:**
- tortilla strips or chips
- sour cream or Greek yogurt
- shredded cheddar or pepper jack cheese

### Method
1. (Optional tortilla strips) Preheat oven to 375°F. Toss tortilla slices with oil, salt, chili powder, and garlic powder on a rimmed baking sheet until evenly coated. Bake for 15–20 minutes until crispy and browned.
2. Add chicken stock, salsa verde, green chiles (not drained), white beans, chicken thighs, zucchini, cumin, chili powder, and smoked paprika to a large pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 30 minutes.
3. Remove the chicken and shred with two forks. Return to the pot with the frozen corn, kale, and cilantro. Cook until the kale is wilted.
4. Taste and adjust salt as needed (saltiness varies depending on stock and beans).
5. Serve topped with tortilla strips or chips, sour cream, cheese, and more cilantro.

### Notes
Shortcut: use pre-cooked chicken or turkey — skip the 30-minute simmer and just heat through.
