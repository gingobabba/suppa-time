### Ingredients
- ground chicken: 1 pound
- white corn, drained: 1 (14-oz) can
- diced green chiles (drain if watery): 1 (4-oz) can
- taco seasoning (ideally sodium-free), divided: 3 teaspoons
- kosher salt, divided: 1 1/8 teaspoons
- cheddar, pepper jack, or Monterey Jack, shredded: 4 ounces (1 cup)
- street taco tortillas, flour or corn: 20
- cooking spray
- mayo, sour cream, or Greek yogurt (or a mix): 1/2 cup
- lime: 1

### Method
1. Position an oven rack in the top third of the oven and preheat to 425°F. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment.
2. Combine ground chicken, corn, green chiles, 2 teaspoons taco seasoning, 1 teaspoon salt, and shredded cheese in a large bowl.
3. If using corn tortillas, wrap the stack in a damp paper towel and microwave for 30 seconds until pliable. Skip this step for flour tortillas.
4. Scoop a spoonful of the raw meat mixture onto half of each tortilla. Fold closed and press. Optionally sprinkle extra cheese on the meat before folding for cheesier tacos. Don't worry if corn tortillas crack slightly — the result will be the same.
5. Arrange tacos on the prepared baking sheets at least 1 inch apart. Spray all over with cooking spray, flip, and spray the second side.
6. Bake for 15–20 minutes until golden-brown and crispy.
7. Make the sauce: stir together the creamy base, remaining 1 teaspoon taco seasoning, juice of half the lime, and 1/8 teaspoon salt.
8. Let tacos cool a few minutes before serving with the sauce.
