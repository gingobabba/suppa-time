### Ingredients

**Lemon vinaigrette:**
- extra-virgin olive oil: 1/2 cup
- lemon juice (about 2 small lemons): 1/2 cup
- Dijon mustard (omit if disliked): 1 heaping tablespoon
- honey: 2 teaspoons
- kosher salt: 1/2 teaspoon
- dried thyme or oregano: 1/4 teaspoon
- garlic powder: 1/4 teaspoon
- black pepper: several grinds

**Salad:**
- uncooked quinoa: 1 cup
- water or stock: 1 1/2 cups
- chopped kale (16-oz bag): 1
- kosher salt and black pepper: to taste
- rotisserie chicken (breast meat only, shredded): 1
- shredded carrots (10-oz bag): 1
- Parmesan, grated: 1 cup (4 oz)
- golden raisins or dried cranberries: 1 cup
- roasted salted almonds, pepitas, or pistachios, chopped: 1 cup
- fresh soft herbs — basil, parsley, or cilantro (optional): 1 cup

### Method
1. Make the dressing: combine all vinaigrette ingredients in a jar and shake until combined. Adjust seasoning to taste. Keeps refrigerated for up to 10 days.
2. Cook quinoa: bring quinoa and water to a boil in a small pot. Reduce to low, cover, and cook for 13–15 minutes until all liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat, keep covered for 5 more minutes, then fluff with a fork. You'll use 2 cups cooked quinoa for this recipe — refrigerate the rest for another use.
3. Add kale to a large bowl and season with a big pinch of salt and pepper. Massage with your hands about 10 times until it just starts to feel damp. Discard any tough stems.
4. Pull and shred the breast meat from the rotisserie chicken. Reserve legs, thighs, and wings in an airtight container in the fridge for another meal within 4 days.
5. Add the shredded chicken, 2 cups cooked quinoa, shredded carrots, Parmesan, and raisins to the kale. Add dressing to taste — about 1/3 cup if serving immediately, less if meal prepping. Toss to coat.
6. Divide into bowls and garnish with more Parmesan, chopped nuts, and a lemon wedge. Add nuts at serving rather than tossing in to keep them from going soggy.

### Notes
Serves 4–6. Consider doubling the quinoa and freezing the extra for future use.
