### Ingredients
- ground chicken: 1 pound
- panko breadcrumbs, plus more for rolling: 1/3 cup
- Parmesan cheese, grated, plus more for garnish: 1/2 cup
- Italian seasoning (or 1 tsp each dried thyme, oregano, and parsley): 1 tablespoon
- kosher salt: 1 teaspoon
- fresh basil, finely chopped, plus more for garnish: 1/4 cup
- garlic cloves, grated (or 1/2 tsp garlic powder): 2
- egg: 1
- cooking spray
- marinara sauce (24-oz jar): 1
- mozzarella (ciliegine, shredded, or burrata): 4 ounces
- French bread, sourdough, cooked pasta, or cauliflower rice: for serving

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. Combine ground chicken, breadcrumbs, Parmesan, Italian seasoning, salt, basil, garlic, and egg in a large bowl. Beat the egg with a fork first, then use your hands to mix everything together.
3. Spray a 10-inch oven-safe skillet with cooking spray. Pour about 1/3 cup panko into a shallow bowl. Form 10 meatballs, roll each in the panko to coat, and place in the skillet. Spray the tops with cooking spray.
4. Bake for 15 minutes. Remove from oven and carefully pour the marinara sauce around the meatballs — the hot skillet may cause some splatter.
5. Scatter mozzarella over the top and return to the oven for 10 minutes, until the cheese is melted.
6. Garnish with fresh basil and Parmesan. Serve immediately from the skillet over bread, pasta, or cauliflower rice.
