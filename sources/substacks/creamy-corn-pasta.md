### Ingredients
- kosher salt: to taste
- farfalle, orecchiette, rigatoni, or cheese tortellini: 1 pound
- large shallot, diced: 1
- garlic cloves, minced: 2
- fresh corn ears, kernels removed (about 2 3/4 cups) OR frozen white corn: 6 ears or 2 (16-oz) bags
- unsalted butter: 4 tablespoons
- red pepper flakes, plus more to taste: 1/8 teaspoon
- water (not pasta water): 1/2 cup
- lemons: 2
- Parmesan cheese, grated, plus more for garnish: 2/3 cup
- fresh basil (half bunch): for garnish

### Method
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook pasta until al dente per package instructions. Before draining, reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water. Drain.
2. Meanwhile, dice the shallot, mince the garlic, and cut the kernels off the corn — lay ears flat on the board to keep kernels from flying.
3. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add shallot, garlic, and red pepper flakes and cook, stirring frequently, for 3–5 minutes until the shallot is translucent. Add corn and 1/2 cup water and cook over medium-high for 4–5 minutes until tender. For frozen corn, add straight from the bag — no thawing needed, may take an extra minute or two.
4. Transfer corn mixture to a blender. Add Parmesan, juice of 1 lemon, and 1 teaspoon salt. Blend on high until smooth, adding reserved pasta water as needed to make it pourable. Taste and adjust with more lemon, salt, or a pinch of sugar if the corn isn't very sweet.
5. Thinly slice the basil leaves.
6. Return drained pasta to the pot. Pour in the corn sauce and a big handful of basil. Toss to combine, adding more pasta water if too thick. Adjust seasoning with lemon, salt, or red pepper flakes.
7. Serve garnished with more basil, Parmesan, and red pepper flakes.

### Notes
If you forget to reserve pasta water, see the source link for a workaround.
