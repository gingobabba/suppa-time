### Ingredients
- sesame oil or neutral cooking oil (e.g. olive oil), divided: 2 tablespoons
- coleslaw mix or shredded cabbage: 2 (10–12 oz) bags
- sliced mushrooms (any kind), finely chopped: 1 (8 oz) container
- scallions, roots trimmed, thinly sliced: 1 bunch
- ground chicken: 1 pound
- kosher salt: to taste
- coconut milk: ⅔ can
- cilantro (leaves and stems), finely chopped: 1 bunch (about 1 packed cup)
- fresh ginger, grated or minced (or ¼ tsp ground ginger): 1-inch piece
- garlic cloves, grated or minced (or ¼ tsp garlic powder): 2
- low-sodium soy sauce: 3 tablespoons
- creamy peanut butter: 1 tablespoon
- fish sauce: 1 tablespoon
- sriracha: 1 tablespoon
- rice vinegar or juice of ½ lime: 2 teaspoons
- honey: 1 teaspoon
- curry powder: ¾ teaspoon
- ground cumin: ¼ teaspoon

### Method
1. In your largest skillet, warm 1 tablespoon oil over medium-high heat. Add coleslaw mix, mushrooms, and scallions. Cook 3–5 minutes until just beginning to soften. Push to one side.
2. Warm remaining 1 tablespoon oil in the empty half. Add ground chicken and cook undisturbed 2–3 minutes, then break into small pieces and cook through, 8–10 minutes total. Stir the cabbage occasionally as the chicken cooks.
3. Once chicken is cooked through, stir everything together. If liquid remains, increase heat to high and stir until evaporated.
4. Reduce to medium-high. Season with a big pinch of salt, then stir in coconut milk, cilantro, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, peanut butter, fish sauce, sriracha, rice vinegar, honey, curry powder, and cumin.
5. Cook 3–5 minutes until sauce thickens and clings to the chicken and vegetables. (If still liquidy after 5 minutes, increase to high heat — it will thicken quickly.)
6. Taste and adjust: salt to pop the flavors, lime/vinegar for tang, honey for sweetness, sriracha for heat.
7. Serve over rice or on its own. Garnish with sriracha, chopped peanuts, and sesame seeds.

### Notes

Avocado oil subbed with olive oil. Serves 4 with rice, 3 without. Quick weeknight bowl.
