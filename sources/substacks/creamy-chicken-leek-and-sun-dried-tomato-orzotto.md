### Ingredients
- boneless, skinless chicken breasts: 1½ pounds
- dried oregano: 1½ teaspoons
- kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper: to taste
- garlic powder: ¾ teaspoon
- red pepper flakes (optional): ¼ to ½ teaspoon
- large leek, thinly sliced and washed: 1
- garlic cloves, minced: 6
- extra-virgin olive oil: 2 tablespoons
- unsalted butter: 2 tablespoons
- low-sodium chicken stock: 3 cups
- uncooked orzo pasta: 16 oz (2½ cups)
- julienned sun-dried tomatoes (oil-packed jar or dry-packed bag): 1½ cups
- heavy cream: 1 pint (2 cups)
- fresh spinach, torn: 2 large handfuls
- lemon (preferably organic), zested: 1
- grated Parmesan, plus more for garnish: ½ cup

### Method
1. Trim fat from chicken breasts and cut into 1-inch cubes. Season with oregano, 1 1/4 teaspoons salt, 1/4 teaspoon pepper, garlic powder, and red pepper flakes if using. Toss to coat on the cutting board.
2. Thinly slice and wash the leek. Mince the garlic.
3. Warm olive oil in a wide skillet or braiser over medium-high heat. Add chicken in an even layer and cook until browned on one side, 2–3 minutes. Add butter, leek, and garlic and stir for 2 minutes until the leek is tender. Move on if the garlic starts to darken before 2 minutes.
4. Stir in chicken stock, orzo, and sun-dried tomatoes (leave the oil behind if using oil-packed). Bring to a boil, then reduce to medium-low. Stir in the heavy cream and simmer for 10–12 minutes, stirring every minute, until the orzo is cooked and the sauce has thickened.
5. Turn off the heat. Tear in the spinach and grate in the lemon zest. Add the Parmesan and stir until the spinach is wilted, 1–2 minutes. If the pasta has tightened up, loosen with a splash of stock or water.
6. Taste and adjust with salt, red pepper flakes, or lemon juice. Serve in bowls garnished with more Parmesan and black pepper.

### Notes

Under 30 minutes. Big batch — serves ~6, good leftover opportunity for a household of 3.
