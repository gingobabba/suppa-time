### Ingredients
- medium shells pasta: 1 pound
- whole milk: 2 1/2 cups
- water: 3 cups
- kosher salt: 2 teaspoons
- garlic powder: 1 teaspoon
- dried thyme: 1/2 teaspoon
- smoked paprika: 1/2 teaspoon
- mustard powder: 1/2 teaspoon
- ground nutmeg: 1/4 teaspoon
- black pepper (freshly ground): 1/4 teaspoon
- canned pumpkin or butternut squash purée: 1 cup
- sharp Cheddar (grated): 8 ounces (about 2 cups)
- smoked Gouda (grated): 4 ounces (about 1 cup)
- breadcrumbs: 3/4 cup

### Method
1. Preheat oven to broil on high.
2. In a large ovenproof pot (such as a Dutch oven), combine pasta, milk, water, salt, garlic powder, thyme, smoked paprika, mustard powder, nutmeg, and pepper.
3. Bring to a simmer over medium-high heat, then reduce to medium. Cook 8–10 minutes until pasta is tender, stirring often to prevent milk from scorching and pasta from sticking.
4. When milk has reduced to the consistency of cream, turn off heat and let cool a couple minutes. Stir in pumpkin purée. Add all the Gouda and all but ½ cup of the Cheddar one handful at a time, stirring to melt. Taste and adjust seasoning.
5. Sprinkle breadcrumbs and remaining ½ cup Cheddar over the top.
6. Broil 2–5 minutes until golden brown and cheese has melted. (Watch closely — broilers vary.)
