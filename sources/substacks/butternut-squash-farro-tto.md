### Ingredients
- extra-virgin olive oil: 2 tablespoons
- yellow onion (large): 1, diced
- farro: 1 1/2 cups
- garlic cloves: 4, minced
- dried oregano: 1 1/2 teaspoons
- low-sodium vegetable broth: 1 quart (4 cups)
- butternut squash (chopped): 3 cups (~20 ounces)
- cauliflower rice: 1 cup (optional)
- kosher salt: to taste
- garlic powder: 1/2 teaspoon
- spinach (chopped): 4 cups
- lemon juice: juice of 1 lemon
- Parmesan cheese (grated): 1 cup
- Gruyère cheese (shredded): 1/2 cup
- frozen broccoli: 1 cup
- black pepper (freshly grated): 1/2 teaspoon

### Method
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Warm olive oil in an ovenproof pot (ideally a cast iron Dutch oven) over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook, stirring frequently, 3–4 minutes until soft. Add farro, garlic, and oregano and cook 1 more minute.
3. Stir in broth, butternut squash, cauliflower rice, 1 teaspoon salt, and garlic powder. Bring to a boil, then turn off the heat.
4. Cover and transfer to the oven. Bake 30–35 minutes until farro is tender and most liquid is absorbed.
5. Smash the squash slightly with the back of a spoon. Stir in spinach, lemon juice, Parmesan, Gruyère, frozen broccoli, and pepper until spinach is wilted and the farro-tto is creamy.
6. Taste and adjust salt and pepper. Serve with extra Parmesan.

### Notes
Peas substituted with frozen broccoli.
